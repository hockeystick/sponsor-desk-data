# Pacific Tower Development

_This sponsor is a fictional analogue. Names, leadership, programs, and figures are invented for synthetic-data purposes and are not affiliated with any real company or organization._

Pacific Tower Development is a privately held real estate developer
based in Portland, Oregon, focused on mixed-use and transit-oriented
residential projects across the metro area and Southwest Washington.
The company was founded in 2008 as a Delaware LLC and is owned by its
two founding partners alongside a Pacific Northwest family-office
equity partner and a Seattle-based public pension allocation taken
on in 2019.

As of February 2026, Pacific Tower carries approximately $1.2 billion
in active development pipeline across Multnomah, Washington, and
Clackamas counties, and a smaller footprint in Clark County,
Washington. The company has delivered 14 completed mid-rise projects
since founding, totaling roughly 2,800 residential units, and
operates with a permanent staff of 38 people out of a Slabtown
office.

## Active and recent projects

- **The Slabtown Yards**. A 312-unit mid-rise on a former rail-yard
  parcel at NW 21st and Wilson, broke ground June 2025. Total
  development cost approximately $148 million. The project includes
  47 affordable units restricted to households at or below 60% of
  area median income, secured under a Multiple-Unit Limited Tax
  Exemption (MULTE) approved by Portland City Council in March 2025.
- **Riverline Commons**. A 220-unit mixed-use building on the
  Vancouver, Washington, waterfront, opened to residents October
  2024. Ground-floor retail tenants include a regional grocer and
  two locally-owned restaurants.
- **Foster-Powell Transit Corridor**. A 188-unit mid-rise along the
  TriMet Line 14 corridor in southeast Portland, currently under
  construction with delivery scheduled for late 2026. The project's
  System Development Charge (SDC) negotiation with the Portland
  Bureau of Transportation extended into a fourteen-month review
  cycle that the company has acknowledged was longer than typical.
- **Lents Industrial Repositioning**. An adaptive-reuse plan for a
  former light-industrial parcel near SE 92nd and Foster, scheduled
  to break ground Q3 2026 pending final permitting.
- **Workforce Housing Fund**. An internal $40 million revolving
  development fund established in 2023, dedicated to projects with
  unit mixes targeting households earning 80–120% of area median
  income. The fund has financed predevelopment work on three
  projects to date.

## Position on contested issues

Pacific Tower Development operates in a publicly contested space, and
the company's communications approach reflects that. The use of
MULTE, tax-increment financing (TIF), and SDC waivers to capitalize
mixed-income projects is a recurring subject in regional housing
investigative coverage, including in The Cascade Tribune. The
company's view, stated by CEO Daniel Reinholt in a January 2026
interview with OPB Think Out Loud, is that the current incentive
stack is the price of any affordable units getting built at the scale
the city actually needs, and that public scrutiny of those
incentives is appropriate and welcome. The company has published
project-level public-benefit accounting on its website since 2023
and updates it quarterly.

In November 2025, Pacific Tower withdrew from the Broadway Tower
office redevelopment in downtown Portland after the project's
financing did not close on terms the company would accept. Reinholt
said publicly at the time that the decision reflected a realistic
read of downtown office vacancy and that pretending otherwise would
have wasted investor and city staff time. The company has stated it
does not intend to underwrite new speculative downtown office
construction in 2026.

In February 2026, the Portland City Auditor released a review of
TIF-supported residential projects over the prior decade. Pacific
Tower had units in three of the audited projects. The audit
identified inconsistent reporting of post-stabilization rent rolls.
The company accepted the finding, has agreed to the auditor's
recommended reporting template, and will publish the corrected
historical data alongside its Q2 2026 quarterly disclosure.
