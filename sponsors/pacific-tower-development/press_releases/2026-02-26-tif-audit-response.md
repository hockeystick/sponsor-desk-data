# Pacific Tower Development accepts findings of City Auditor's TIF residential review, will adopt new reporting template

*Portland, Oregon — February 26, 2026.* Pacific Tower Development
issued a written response today to the Portland City Auditor's
February 2026 review of tax-increment-financed residential projects
over the prior decade. The company accepted the audit's findings as
they relate to its three audited projects and committed to adopting
the auditor's recommended post-stabilization rent-roll reporting
template effective Q2 2026.

The auditor's review, released February 19, 2026, examined 31
TIF-supported residential projects citywide and identified
inconsistent reporting of post-stabilization rent rolls as a
program-wide finding. Pacific Tower had unit interests in three of
the audited projects: a 2014 RiverPlace-area mid-rise, a 2017
Slabtown delivery, and a 2020 outer Southeast project.

"The auditor's finding is correct, and our response is to fix it on
the timeline the auditor recommended," said Marcus Whitfield, vice
president for public affairs at Pacific Tower Development. "We are
adopting the recommended reporting template, we are publishing the
corrected historical data with our Q2 2026 quarterly disclosure, and
we are not contesting any portion of the finding as it applies to
us."

Pacific Tower chief executive officer Daniel Reinholt addressed the
February 26 City Council session at which the audit was discussed.
"TIF is a deliberate policy choice, and the public has a real
interest in knowing whether the rent restrictions in TIF-supported
projects held over time," Reinholt said. "When the data we publish
makes that question harder to answer, that is on us, and we fix it."

The corrected historical data will cover the three audited Pacific
Tower projects from their respective stabilization dates through the
end of 2025 and will follow the auditor's recommended template. The
company will publish the data alongside its existing quarterly
public-benefit disclosure on the company website. Pacific Tower's
chief operating officer, Priya Anand, will sign the disclosure.

The company will also revise its internal post-stabilization
reporting workflow for active and future projects, including The
Slabtown Yards and the Foster-Powell Transit Corridor, to apply the
new template from the date of stabilization.
