# Pacific Tower Development withdraws from Broadway Tower downtown office redevelopment

*Portland, Oregon — November 18, 2025.* Pacific Tower Development
notified the property owner and the city this week that the company
is withdrawing from the Broadway Tower redevelopment project, an
adaptive-reuse proposal for a vacant downtown office building near
SW Broadway and Yamhill. The withdrawal follows the failure of the
project's debt and equity stack to close on terms the company would
accept.

The Broadway Tower project, first announced in February 2024, would
have converted approximately 18 floors of vacant Class B office
space to a mix of residential units and ground-floor retail. The
proposal had received preliminary land-use approval and was awaiting
final financing close.

"Downtown office vacancy is what the data says it is, and the lender
appetite for a conversion of this size at this rate environment is
what the term sheets we received say it is," said Daniel Reinholt,
chief executive officer of Pacific Tower Development. "We were not
going to commit investor capital, or city staff time, to a project
the underlying numbers do not support. Pretending otherwise would
have been a more expensive mistake later."

The decision affects approximately $11 million in predevelopment
expenditure, which Pacific Tower will absorb. Approximately 220
projected residential units will not be delivered. The property
remains vacant and listed for sale; the property owner has indicated
it will pursue alternative redevelopment partners.

Pacific Tower vice president for public affairs Marcus Whitfield said
the company has notified the Portland Bureau of Planning and the
office of the city's economic recovery coordinator and will not seek
recovery of any city staff time invested in the predevelopment
review.

"We owe the planners and reviewers who put time into this project
straight news, not a stalled file," Whitfield said. "We have told
them directly that we are not coming back to this site in 2026."

Reinholt said the company will continue to evaluate downtown
adaptive-reuse projects on a case-by-case basis but does not intend
to underwrite new speculative downtown office construction in 2026.
The company's residential pipeline in Slabtown, Foster-Powell, Lents,
and the Vancouver, Washington, waterfront is unaffected by the
Broadway Tower decision.
