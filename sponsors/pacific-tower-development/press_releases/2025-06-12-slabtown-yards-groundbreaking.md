# Pacific Tower Development breaks ground on 312-unit Slabtown Yards mixed-use project

*Portland, Oregon — June 12, 2025.* Pacific Tower Development held a
groundbreaking ceremony this morning for The Slabtown Yards, a
312-unit mixed-use mid-rise on a former rail-yard parcel at NW 21st
and Wilson. Construction is expected to take approximately 26 months,
with first move-ins scheduled for fall 2027.

Total development cost is approximately $148 million. The project
includes 47 units restricted to households at or below 60% of area
median income, secured through a Multiple-Unit Limited Tax Exemption
(MULTE) approved by Portland City Council in March 2025. Ground-floor
space along NW 21st will house approximately 14,000 square feet of
retail, with leasing inquiries handled by the company's brokerage
partner.

"This site has been a hole in the Slabtown street wall for fifteen
years, and the units we are putting on it include 47 that will rent
to working households at or below 60% AMI for the next ten years,"
said Daniel Reinholt, chief executive officer and co-founder of
Pacific Tower Development. "The MULTE made those units financially
possible. The taxing districts are forgoing real revenue to make that
happen. Both halves of that bargain deserve to be stated out loud."

Pacific Tower's chief operating officer, Priya Anand, said the
construction sequence has been planned in coordination with the
Slabtown Neighborhood Association and adjacent property owners. The
project will use a single staging lane on NW Wilson, restricted truck
hours of 7 a.m. to 5 p.m. on weekdays, and a noise-monitoring
program reporting weekly to the city's Bureau of Development
Services.

"The neighbors are going to live with this construction for 26
months," Anand said. "They get biweekly construction-update emails,
they get named office hours twice a month, and they get a phone line
in Spanish and English. That is a baseline obligation, not a
sponsorship line item."

The project's general contractor is a Portland-based firm whose
selection process included evaluation criteria for apprenticeship
hiring, women- and minority-owned subcontractor inclusion, and
prevailing-wage compliance documentation.

The Slabtown Yards is the largest single project in Pacific Tower
Development's active pipeline. The company will publish quarterly
construction progress reports on its website beginning Q3 2025.
