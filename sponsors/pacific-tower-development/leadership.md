# Leadership — Pacific Tower Development

This is an internal sales-desk reference document. All quotes below
are drawn from public statements; none of the bios reflect private

## Daniel Reinholt, Chief Executive Officer and Co-founder

Reinholt co-founded Pacific Tower in 2008 with chief financial
partner Adam Yelvington (since retired). Previously a project
director at Williams & Dame Development, where he led the
predevelopment phase on two RiverPlace-area mid-rises; before that,
an associate at Holland Partner Group's Portland office. Holds a
BArch from the University of Oregon (2001) and an MBA from
Willamette University (2007). In a January 2026 OPB Think Out Loud
appearance, Reinholt acknowledged the company's MULTE participation
"is a deliberate policy bargain — not a gift either direction" and
defended continued participation while welcoming public scrutiny.

## Priya Anand, Chief Operating Officer

Anand joined Pacific Tower in 2017 and was promoted to COO in 2021.
Previously vice president for development at Trammell Crow Northwest,
where she ran the firm's suburban multifamily pipeline across
Washington and Clackamas counties. Holds a BA in economics from Reed
College and a Master of Urban Planning from Portland State. Anand
oversees construction operations, vendor management, and the
Workforce Housing Fund. In a November 2025 Portland Business Journal
interview, she said the Foster-Powell project's extended SDC review
"was the system working, not failing — but the timeline still cost
us."

## Marcus Whitfield, Vice President for Public Affairs

Whitfield joined Pacific Tower in 2021. Previously chief of staff to
a Portland city commissioner (2016–2021), where his portfolio
included planning, transportation, and small-business policy. Earlier
roles include legislative aide in the Oregon House of Representatives
and policy fellow at 1000 Friends of Oregon. Holds a BA from Lewis &
Clark College and a JD from Lewis & Clark Law School. Whitfield is
the company's primary contact for media relations and civic
partnership conversations. He represented the company at the
February 2026 City Council session on the auditor's TIF review.

## Helena Ostrowski, Senior Vice President for Finance

Ostrowski joined Pacific Tower in 2019 from a regional commercial
banking role at Umpqua Bank, where she ran the construction-lending
desk for the Portland metro market. Earlier career at JPMorgan's real
estate finance group in Seattle. BS in finance from the University of
Oregon. She is the company's signatory on debt facilities and
project-level limited partner agreements.

## Jacob Tran, Director of Marketing and Communications

Tran joined Pacific Tower in 2023. Previously communications manager
at Prosper Portland (2018–2023), and before that a reporter at
Willamette Week covering housing and zoning. Holds a BA in journalism
from the University of Oregon. Tran handles press contacts,
underwriting conversations, and the company's quarterly
public-benefit disclosure. He is the named press contact on most
public statements.

## Decision-makers for sponsorship decisions (2026)

For sponsorships and underwriting partnerships, the decision tree is
as follows. Underwriting and event sponsorships under $25,000:
Director of Marketing and Communications (Jacob Tran) approves
directly. Partnerships from $25,000 to $100,000: requires SVP for
Finance (Helena Ostrowski) sign-off and a written firewall
acknowledgment from the publication. Partnerships above $100,000,
multi-year underwriting agreements, and any sponsorship touching
zoning, TIF, or housing-policy beats: require CEO (Daniel Reinholt)
sign-off and Executive Committee notification.
