# Portland Museum of Art expands Title I Free Day program with transportation grant

*Portland, Oregon — August 21, 2025.* The Portland Museum of Art
today announced an expansion of its Title I Free Day program
adding a $180 per bus-day transportation grant for participating
schools, capped at 200 buses per year. The expansion takes effect
with the 2025–26 school year and adds approximately $36,000 in
program costs, funded through the museum's operating budget.

The Title I Free Day program, established in 2018, provides free
admission and a curriculum-aligned docent tour for every Title I
school in the Portland Public Schools, David Douglas, Reynolds,
Centennial, and Parkrose districts. The program served
approximately 9,400 students in the 2024–25 school year across
112 school visits.

The transportation grant addresses a finding from the program's
three-year evaluation, completed in spring 2025, that bus costs
rather than admission were the binding constraint for
participating schools. Of the 41 Title I schools eligible for the
program in the 2024–25 school year, 14 — primarily in the David
Douglas and Reynolds districts — reported that they had been
unable to participate in some or all years because of bus
funding gaps in the district transportation budget.

"Free admission isn't free if a school can't pay for the bus,"
Director of Education Kenji Howe said. "We had been measuring
the wrong number."

The grant is structured as a direct reimbursement to the
participating school's district transportation office on receipt
of an invoice and tour-completion confirmation. The 200-bus
annual cap was set based on the program's projected 2025–26
demand and is reviewable in the 2026–27 cycle.

Director Eleanor Tagaq noted that the program expansion is being
funded out of operating revenue rather than the museum's
restricted endowment, and is included in the $14 million Access
Endowment campaign now under way. The Access Endowment, when
fully funded, would make the Title I Free Day program and the
museum's two-free-Sundays-per-month admission policy permanent
regardless of single-year operating performance.

The program's enrollment window for the 2025–26 school year is
open through October 15, 2025, with rolling availability for
spring-term tours. Information for school administrators is
available on the museum's education website.
