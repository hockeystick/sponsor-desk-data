# Portland Museum of Art names Dr. Andrea Marsh-Onuoha as Senior Curator of Pacific Northwest Painting

*Portland, Oregon — March 12, 2026.* The Portland Museum of Art
today announced the appointment of Dr. Andrea Marsh-Onuoha as
Senior Curator of Pacific Northwest Painting, a newly established
position within the museum's curatorial department. Marsh-Onuoha
will join the museum on June 1, 2026, from the Walker Art Center
in Minneapolis, where she has served as associate curator of
painting and sculpture since 2019.

The position is the museum's first dedicated senior curatorial
chair for Pacific Northwest painting and is intended to anchor
the museum's curatorial direction in 20th- and 21st-century
regional painting for the next decade. The search, opened in
October 2025, drew 94 applicants and was conducted by a committee
chaired by Chief Curator Marisol Reyes.

"This is a 10-year direction, not a single hire," Director
Eleanor Tagaq said. "Andrea's scholarship on the Northwest
School and on contemporary regional painters who are working
outside that frame is the work we want to anchor on."

Marsh-Onuoha's scholarly work includes a 2022 monograph on the
painter Kenneth Callahan published by the University of
Washington Press and a 2024 essay collection on contemporary
Pacific Northwest figurative painting that she co-edited with
SFMOMA curator Aimee Park. Before the Walker, she was assistant
curator at the Tacoma Art Museum from 2014 to 2019; she holds a
PhD in art history from the University of California, Berkeley
and a BA from Oberlin.

Marsh-Onuoha will lead the curatorial program for the Rothko
Pavilion's second-cycle exhibitions, beginning in fall 2027,
following the Pavilion's inaugural regional commission opening
in the fourth quarter of 2026. Her first exhibition for the
museum, drawn from the permanent collection, will open in spring
2027.

"The collection has depth in painters whose work has not had a
sustained scholarly treatment in recent decades — Sally
Haley, George Johanson, Lucinda Parker among others," Marsh-
Onuoha said. "I'm coming to Portland to do that scholarly
work, in partnership with a curatorial department that has
already done significant groundwork."

The position is funded through a permanent curatorial chair
established by an anonymous gift to the museum in 2024.
Marsh-Onuoha will report to Chief Curator Marisol Reyes.
