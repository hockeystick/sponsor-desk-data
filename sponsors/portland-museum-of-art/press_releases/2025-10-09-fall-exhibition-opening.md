# Portland Museum of Art opens "Watershed: Six Artists at the Confluence" October 18

*Portland, Oregon — October 9, 2025.* The Portland Museum of Art
today announced the opening of "Watershed: Six Artists at the
Confluence," the second exhibition drawn from the museum's
2025–27 PMA Regional Commissions cohort. The exhibition opens
to the public on Saturday, October 18, 2025, and runs through
February 22, 2026, in the museum's Belluschi Building third-floor
galleries.

The PMA Regional Commissions program is a biennial commissioning
program for Pacific Northwest artists, providing each
commissioned artist with a $45,000 fee, exhibition, and a printed
catalogue produced by the museum's curatorial department. The
2025–27 cohort consists of six artists working across painting,
sculpture, video, and fiber.

"Watershed" features new work by:

- Aiyana Pike (Confederated Tribes of Warm Springs), large-scale
  cedar-bark and copper sculpture;
- Marcus Olufemi (Portland), oil paintings on Columbia River
  geography;
- Eun-Joo Park (Seattle), single-channel video drawn from
  archival footage of the 1996 Willamette flood;
- Sara Beltrán (Hood River), woven works incorporating
  invasive-species fiber;
- Tomás Reyna (Eugene), oil paintings of Eastern Oregon
  high-desert ranching;
- Lila Ostroff (Portland), ceramic and glass works on Pacific
  Northwest river ecology.

Chief Curator Marisol Reyes, who organized the exhibition, said
in a statement that the cohort was selected from 187 applicants
through a panel review that included two non-museum jurors —
artist Storm Tharp and Crow's Shadow Institute of the Arts
director Karl Davis. The catalogue, with essays by Reyes and
guest contributors, is published October 14 by the museum.

Admission is free with general museum admission; the exhibition
is included in the museum's two free Sundays per month
(November 2 and 16; December 7 and 21).

A public conversation with three of the cohort artists is
scheduled for Thursday, October 23, at 6:30 p.m. in the Mark
Building's Crumpacker Family Library. Registration is open at
the museum's events page.
