# Leadership — Portland Museum of Art

_This sponsor is a fictional analogue. Names, leadership, programs, and figures are invented for synthetic-data purposes and are not affiliated with any real company or organization._

The Portland Museum of Art is governed by a 27-member volunteer
board of trustees and led by an executive team of five, supported
by a curatorial staff of fourteen and a total museum staff of
approximately 180.

## Dr. Eleanor Tagaq, Director and CEO

Tagaq was appointed Director and CEO in August 2022 following a
ten-month search. She came to the museum from the Seattle Asian
Art Museum, where she served as deputy director for four years;
earlier in her career she was curator of contemporary art at the
Anchorage Museum. Tagaq is of Iñupiaq heritage, holds a PhD in
art history from the University of Washington, and graduated from
Reed College with a BA in art history.

In a March 2024 statement accompanying the board's adoption of
the museum's repatriation policy, Tagaq said: "The federal
statute is the minimum the law requires of us. It is not the
maximum we owe the tribes whose objects we hold."

## Marisol Reyes, Chief Curator

Reyes joined the museum in 2020 from SFMOMA, where she was
associate curator of painting and sculpture for six years. She
authored the catalogue for the museum's 2024 retrospective on
Lucinda Parker and is leading curatorial planning for the Rothko
Pavilion's inaugural exhibitions. Reyes holds a PhD in art history
from Columbia and an MA from Williams.

In a January 2026 interview with Art in America, Reyes said:
"The Pavilion's first show should announce a direction, not
borrow one. A traveling blockbuster does the opposite."

## Kenji Howe, Director of Education

Howe has been at the museum for twelve years and designed the
original Title I Free Day program in 2018. He led the 2025
expansion that added the $180 per bus-day transportation grant
after the program's three-year evaluation found that bus costs,
not admission, were the binding constraint for participating
schools.

In an August 2025 statement at the program-expansion
announcement, Howe said: "Free admission isn't free if a school
can't pay for the bus. We had been measuring the wrong number."

## Vivian Brand-Olsen, Director of Development

Brand-Olsen joined the museum in 2021 from Literary Arts, where
she was development director for seven years. She is leading the
$120 million Rothko Pavilion capital campaign, of which $108
million had been committed as of February 2026, and the
$14 million Access Endowment, of which $4.6 million had been
committed as of the same date.

## David Hayslett, Chair, Board of Trustees

Hayslett, a retired Portland-based attorney, was elected board
chair in June 2024. He has served on the board since 2017 and
chaired the Rothko Pavilion design-review subcommittee that
oversaw the 2024 design revision adding the at-grade ADA
entrance.

## Decision-makers for sponsorship decisions (2026)

Sponsorship and underwriting decisions sit in the museum's
Development office under Director of Development Vivian
Brand-Olsen. Sponsorships under $25,000 are approved by
Brand-Olsen and the relevant program director. Sponsorships
between $25,000 and $100,000 require sign-off from the Director
and the Director of Development jointly. Sponsorships over
$100,000, and any sponsorship that names an exhibition or
program, require approval by the board of trustees' development
committee.

Curatorial decisions — including artist selection, exhibition
title, and catalogue text — are made by the Chief Curator under
delegated authority from the Director, and are not subject to
sponsor input under any sponsorship agreement the museum will
enter.
