# 2026 Brand Priorities — Onpoint Cooperative

_This sponsor is a fictional analogue. Names, leadership, programs, and figures are invented for synthetic-data purposes and are not affiliated with any real company or organization._

_Internal document shared with regional media partners and civic stakeholders. Approved by the Board Communications Committee, February 2026._

Onpoint Cooperative's 2026 communications agenda is built around
delivery on programs already announced rather than on new program
launches. The cooperative is in the second year of its Branch
Reinvestment Initiative, the fifth year of Oregon Homefront, and
the first full year of its NAYA community-development partnership.
The priorities below reflect what we are accountable for this year.

## 1. Grow Oregon Homefront in census tracts where the data says it matters

Oregon Homefront has funded approximately 1,400 loans since 2022,
with average assistance of $19,200. The 2026 plan targets 600
additional loans, with at least 60% of those in census tracts that
the program's eligibility model identifies as having the highest
gap between qualified borrower demand and observed first-time
homebuyer activity. We will publish geographic distribution data
quarterly beginning Q2 2026.

## 2. Double the EV auto loan portfolio

The EV Drive Forward Auto Loan portfolio finished 2025 at
approximately $94 million in outstanding balances. The 2026 plan
targets a year-end portfolio of $190 million, supported by a
modest expansion of certified-used eligibility and a partnership
with three Oregon-based EV dealerships on member-pricing referrals.
The rate reduction structure (75 basis points on qualifying new
and certified-used EVs) does not change.

## 3. Finish the Astoria and Hood River branch rebuilds on schedule

The Branch Reinvestment Initiative's first two completed projects
— Astoria and Hood River — are scheduled to reopen in May and
August 2026, respectively. Both projects ran into supply-chain
delays in 2025 that we did not initially communicate well to
members in those communities. The 2026 plan includes monthly
member updates from the project lead and named-staff information
sessions during the four weeks before each reopening.

## 4. Expand financial literacy programming into East Portland and Salem

The Members First Financial Literacy series ran 142 sessions in 2025
across 18 library partners. The 2026 plan adds five new partner
locations, prioritizing East Portland (Midland and Holgate libraries)
and Salem (Salem Public Library's east-side branch). Sessions in
those locations will be offered in English and Spanish; the Cully
location will additionally include Somali-language sessions in
partnership with a community organization the credit union will
name when the agreement is signed.

## 5. Deliver on the NAYA community development partnership

The community development partnership with the Native American Youth
and Family Center, announced March 2026, includes a three-year $4.5
million commitment from the credit union supporting NAYA's
homebuyer-readiness and small-business technical-assistance
programming. The 2026 deliverable is the first cohort of homebuyer
clients moving from NAYA's program into Oregon Homefront-supported
mortgage closings, with quarterly progress reports issued jointly
by Onpoint and NAYA.

## What we will not do

- We will not run brand campaigns that frame the 2025 member
  dividend as a uniquely large return. It is a meaningful return
  to members; it is also a recurring annual rebate.
- We will not underwrite news coverage of credit union industry
  regulation, NCUA examination findings (ours or peers'), or
  state-level consumer-finance legislation.
- We will not close additional branches in 2026 without 90-day
  notice and in-person community information sessions. The
  Tillamook 2023 timeline was the wrong precedent.
