# Leadership — Onpoint Cooperative

_This sponsor is a fictional analogue. Names, leadership, programs, and figures are invented for synthetic-data purposes and are not affiliated with any real company or organization._

This is an internal sales-desk reference document. All quotes below

## Linnea Tørgersen, President and Chief Executive Officer

Tørgersen joined Onpoint as chief financial officer in 2017 and was
named president and CEO in January 2020. Previously chief financial
officer at an Oregon-based community credit union (2013–2017);
before that, an examiner with the National Credit Union
Administration's Pacific Region (2008–2013). Holds a BS in finance
from Portland State and an MBA from the University of Oregon. In a
December 2025 letter to members, Tørgersen committed the board to
publishing a written allocation rationale for the 2026 dividend
alongside its declaration.

## Andrew Brightwater, Chief Lending Officer

Brightwater has been at Onpoint for twenty-two years, joining in
2003 as a mortgage underwriter and rising through credit risk and
consumer-lending leadership before being named chief lending officer
in 2018. Holds a BBA from Oregon State. In a February 2026 trade
press interview, Brightwater described the credit union's revised
used-vehicle underwriting policy as "a response to data, not a
posture."

## Janelle Two Crows, Vice President for Community Development

Two Crows joined Onpoint in 2023 from the Native American Youth and
Family Center (NAYA), where she directed economic-development
programs from 2018 to 2023. Earlier roles include policy associate
at Oregon Housing and Community Services and program coordinator at
the Cully Boulevard Alliance. Holds a BA from Lewis & Clark College
and a Master of Social Work from Portland State. Two Crows is the
co-author, with NAYA's executive director, of the March 2026
partnership agreement that anchors the credit union's 2026
community-development priorities.

## Daniel Mahoney, Chief Marketing Officer

Mahoney joined Onpoint in 2022. Previously vice president for
member experience at a Northern California credit union; earlier
roles include marketing director at Travel Oregon's regional
operations team. Holds a BA in communications from the University of
Oregon. Mahoney oversees member communications, sponsorships,
underwriting partnerships, and the credit union's quarterly member
publication.

## Reuben Patel, Senior Vice President for Communications and Government Affairs

Patel joined Onpoint in 2021. Previously deputy chief of staff to a
member of the Oregon legislature, where his portfolio included
financial services and consumer protection; before that, a research
associate at the Oregon Department of Consumer and Business
Services. Holds a BA from the University of Oregon and a Master of
Public Policy from Portland State. Patel is the credit union's
primary contact for press inquiries, OPB and KOIN appearances, and
state government relations.

## Decision-makers for sponsorship decisions (2026)

For sponsorships and underwriting partnerships, the decision tree is
as follows. Underwriting and event sponsorships under $25,000:
Director of Member Communications (reports to the CMO) approves
directly. Partnerships from $25,000 to $100,000: requires Chief
Marketing Officer (Daniel Mahoney) sign-off. Partnerships above
$100,000, multi-year underwriting agreements, and any sponsorship
touching credit union industry regulation, NCUA matters, or
state-level consumer-finance legislation: requires SVP for
Communications and Government Affairs (Reuben Patel) sign-off and
CEO (Linnea Tørgersen) review.
