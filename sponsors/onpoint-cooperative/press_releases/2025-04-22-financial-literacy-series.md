# Onpoint Cooperative launches Members First Financial Literacy series across 18 Oregon and Southwest Washington libraries

*Portland, Oregon — April 22, 2025.* Onpoint Cooperative announced
today the launch of its Members First Financial Literacy series, a
year-long workshop program delivering one-hour sessions on credit,
budgeting, homebuying, and student-loan repayment at 18 partner
libraries across Oregon and Southwest Washington.

The program is structured as a season-long sequence at each partner
location, with the same staff returning to the same library across
multiple sessions rather than as one-off appearances. Partner
libraries include Multnomah County Library (six branches), Salem
Public Library, Hood River County Library, Deschutes Public Library
in Bend, the Astoria Public Library, and three Fort Vancouver
Regional Library branches in Southwest Washington.

"Showing up at a library twice a year is not a financial literacy
program," said Janelle Two Crows, vice president for community
development at Onpoint Cooperative. "We had to rebuild this to be a
season-long sequence in the same place with the same staff. The
people walking into a financial literacy session are bringing real
questions, and answering them takes more than a single slide deck."

Sessions are open to the public and do not require Onpoint
membership. Workshop topics for the 2025 program year include credit
report basics, household budgeting, first-time homebuyer financial
preparation, student loan repayment options under current federal
rules, and an introduction to credit union ownership structure for
members and prospective members.

The 2025 program will run approximately 140 sessions across the
partner network. Onpoint staff facilitating the sessions are full-
time credit union employees who hold financial counselor
certifications; the program does not use sales staff or product-tied
incentives. Workshops are offered in English at all locations and in
Spanish at six locations, with additional language partnerships in
development for 2026.

Onpoint chief marketing officer Daniel Mahoney said the program is
funded out of the credit union's community development budget and
not out of marketing spend.

"This is a member-owned cooperative running a financial literacy
program because that is part of what cooperatives do," Mahoney said.
"It is not a marketing channel and we have asked our team to keep
it that way."

The full 2025 schedule is available on the Onpoint Cooperative
website.
