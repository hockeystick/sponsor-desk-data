# Onpoint Cooperative and Native American Youth and Family Center announce three-year community development partnership

*Portland, Oregon — March 18, 2026.* Onpoint Cooperative and the
Native American Youth and Family Center (NAYA) announced today a
three-year community development partnership under which the credit
union will provide $4.5 million in support of NAYA's homebuyer-
readiness and small-business technical-assistance programs over
2026 through 2028.

The agreement, signed at NAYA's Cully campus on March 17, 2026,
includes annual financial commitments of $1.5 million each year and
a programmatic component connecting NAYA homebuyer-readiness program
graduates to the credit union's Oregon Homefront down-payment
assistance product. The partnership also funds two NAYA staff
positions dedicated to small-business loan readiness and a quarterly
joint progress report issued by both organizations.

"The measure of this partnership is closings, not photos," said
Linnea Tørgersen, president and chief executive officer of Onpoint
Cooperative. "We will publish, jointly with NAYA, the number of
homebuyer-readiness graduates each quarter, the number who proceed
to mortgage application, and the number who close. The first cohort
moving through that pipeline is the 2026 deliverable."

The partnership originated from work begun by Onpoint vice president
for community development Janelle Two Crows, who joined the credit
union in 2023 after directing economic-development programs at NAYA.

"This is a partnership between two organizations that have been in
the same room on the same questions for years," Two Crows said.
"What is new is the structure: a three-year written commitment, a
specific dollar amount, and a quarterly accountability report that
both organizations sign."

NAYA's executive director said in a joint statement that the
agreement reflects "a serious commitment to outcomes" and that the
small-business technical-assistance component will support NAYA's
existing entrepreneurship programs serving Native-owned businesses
in the Portland metro area.

The Oregon Homefront down-payment assistance product, launched in
2022, has funded approximately 1,400 first-time homebuyer loans
through the end of 2025, with average assistance of $19,200. Eligible
borrowers from NAYA's homebuyer-readiness program will be referred
to Onpoint mortgage staff trained in the program's underwriting
guidelines beginning April 1, 2026.

The full text of the partnership agreement, including programmatic
deliverables and reporting schedule, is available on the Onpoint
Cooperative website.
