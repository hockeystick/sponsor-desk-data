# Onpoint Cooperative

_This sponsor is a fictional analogue. Names, leadership, programs, and figures are invented for synthetic-data purposes and are not affiliated with any real company or organization._

Onpoint Cooperative is a member-owned credit union serving
approximately 480,000 members across Oregon, Southwest Washington,
and a small footprint in southern Idaho. As of year-end 2025, Onpoint
held approximately $11.1 billion in assets and operated 55
full-service branches, with the heaviest concentration in
Multnomah, Washington, and Clackamas counties and additional
branches in Vancouver, Camas, and Battle Ground in Washington and in
Hood River, Bend, Salem, Eugene, and Astoria in Oregon.

The credit union was chartered in 1932 as Beaverton-Tek Federal
Credit Union, originally serving electrical engineers at the early
Tek facilities in Washington County. The credit union expanded to a
community charter in 1998 covering Washington, Multnomah, and
Clackamas counties; the charter was further expanded in 2007 to
cover the rest of Oregon and Southwest Washington. The cooperative
adopted its current name, Onpoint Cooperative, in 2003.

## Products

- **Oregon Homefront**. A first-time homebuyer down-payment assistance
  product launched in 2022, offering up to $25,000 in forgivable
  second-position assistance for borrowers in eligible Oregon census
  tracts. The product had funded approximately 1,400 loans by the
  end of 2025, with an average assistance amount of $19,200.
- **EV Drive Forward Auto Loan**. A 75-basis-point rate reduction on
  qualifying new and certified-used electric vehicle auto loans, with
  a separate 50-basis-point reduction for qualifying plug-in hybrid
  vehicles.
- **Members First Financial Literacy**. A community workshop series
  launched in 2024, delivering one-hour sessions on credit, budgeting,
  homebuying, and student-loan repayment. The 2025 program ran 142
  sessions across 18 Oregon and Southwest Washington libraries,
  including partnerships with Multnomah County Library and Salem
  Public Library.
- **Branch Reinvestment Initiative**. A five-branch refresh and
  rebuild program running 2025–2027, prioritizing Beaverton,
  Hillsboro, Hood River, Bend, and Astoria. Total projected spend
  is approximately $34 million.
- **Member Dividend**. The credit union's annual patronage rebate.
  In December 2025, the board declared a 2025 member dividend of
  approximately $58 million, returned to members in proportion to
  share balances and qualifying loan activity during the year.

## Leadership

- **Linnea Tørgersen**, President and Chief Executive Officer since
  January 2020. Previously chief financial officer of an Oregon-based
  community credit union; before that, an examiner with the National
  Credit Union Administration's Pacific Region. Holds a BS in
  finance from Portland State and an MBA from the University of

- **Andrew Brightwater**, Chief Lending Officer. Twenty-two years at

  mortgage lending. Holds a BBA from Oregon State.

  Joined Onpoint in 2023 from the Native American Youth and Family

  Holds an MSW from Portland State.

The credit union is governed by a nine-member volunteer board of
directors elected by the membership. Three board seats are up for
election each year on rotating three-year terms. The board's audit,
risk, and community-development committees each include at least
one member who is not also on the board's executive committee.

## Position on contested issues

Onpoint's 2025 member dividend, declared at $58 million, drew
critique from two member groups during the December 2025 annual
meeting. One group asked the board to direct a larger share of
2025 net income to community-reinvestment grants rather than to
patronage rebates. A second group asked for a larger per-member
dividend at the expense of branch reinvestment spending. Chief
executive Linnea Tørgersen, in her response at the meeting,
acknowledged that the cooperative form does require those
allocations to compete and said the 2026 board would publish a
written allocation rationale alongside the next dividend declaration.

In a higher rate environment, the credit union's auto-lending
volumes have grown faster than its mortgage volumes, and member
default rates on used-vehicle loans rose modestly through 2025. The
credit union's response, announced February 2026, includes a
restructured underwriting policy for used-vehicle loans above 72
months, a member outreach program for borrowers showing early
delinquency signals, and an expanded financial coaching offering
that does not require enrollment in a fee product.

In November 2023 the credit union closed its Tillamook branch, a
location it had operated since 2009, citing sustained low transaction
volume. The closure drew member objections in the local press. The
credit union's December 2023 written response stated the closure was
the right decision but that the timing — announced six weeks before
closure — had been too short. Subsequent branch decisions, including
a 2025 hours-reduction at the company's Eugene location, have
followed a 90-day notice protocol with in-person community
information sessions.
