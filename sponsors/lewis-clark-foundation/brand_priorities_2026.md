# 2026 Programmatic Priorities - Lewis & Clark Foundation

_This sponsor is a fictional analogue. Names, leadership, programs, and figures are invented for synthetic-data purposes and are not affiliated with any real company or organization._

_Internal document shared with regional media partners and civic stakeholders. Approved by the Foundation Board of Trustees, December 2025._

The foundation's 2026 priorities continue the program lines
established in the 2024-2027 strategic plan, with two specific
expansions tied to the September 2025 opening of the Riverbend
Environmental Law Center and the February 2026 launch of the OHSU
climate-health research partnership. Distribution targets below are
approved by the board and reflect the foundation's 5.0 percent
five-year average payout rate.

## 1. Environmental Law Clinic: full operations in the Riverbend center

The Riverbend Environmental Law Center opened in September 2025.
The 2026 budget funds the clinic at full operating capacity in the
new building, including six staff attorneys, three clinical
fellows, a clinic administrator, and approximately 24 law students
per academic year. Operating support flows from the Environmental
Law Clinic Endowment at a 4.5 percent annual draw, generating
approximately $2.16 million for 2026 operations.

The board reaffirmed in December 2025 that the foundation will not
amend the 1989 clinic governance agreement that places case
selection with the supervising attorneys. The position was tested
in 2025 by the pesticide-drift case in Marion County Circuit Court,
which is expected to continue into the 2026-27 academic year.

## 2. Public Service Fellowship program: 2026 cohort of 18 fellows

The 2026 cohort will be 18 fellows, the same size as the 2025
cohort. Stipends are increased to $44,000 (from $42,000) reflecting
metro-Portland cost-of-living changes, with partial tuition
remission held flat at 50 percent of in-program tuition. Fellows
will continue to commit to two years of post-graduation public-sector
or nonprofit work in Oregon. The 2026 application cycle opens in
January and closes March 4.

## 3. First-Generation Programs: expand emergency-aid fund

The First-Generation Programs office reported in October 2025 that
its emergency-aid fund had been fully drawn down by the third week
of the fall semester for the second consecutive year. The 2026
budget adds $250,000 to the emergency-aid line, bringing the total
to $475,000, and shifts disbursement authority from a single
director sign-off to a three-person committee with a 48-hour
turnaround commitment.

## 4. OHSU climate-health research partnership

The foundation will commit $1.5 million in 2026 toward a three-year,
$4.5 million joint research partnership with Oregon Health &
Science University and the Oregon Climate Change Research Institute
focused on climate-health intersections, specifically wildfire-smoke
exposure and vector-borne disease shifts in Oregon. The partnership
was announced in February 2026 and is structured as a co-funded
research consortium with formal data-sharing protocols and
quarterly published findings.

## What we will not do

- We will not amend the Environmental Law Clinic's case-selection
  governance. The September 2025 board reaffirmation settles the
  question for the 2026 program year.
- We will not redirect endowment payout to short-term capital
  projects. The Riverbend center was funded with a separate capital
  campaign and reserve authorization; the operating endowment is
  not a project-funding vehicle and the board has declined two
  donor-advised proposals in 2025 that would have moved in that
  direction.
- We will not enter naming-rights arrangements that would condition
  research-program design on donor preferences. The 1989 governance
  framework that protects clinic case selection extends, by board
  policy, to research-partnership agenda-setting.
