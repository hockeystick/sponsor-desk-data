# Lewis & Clark Foundation, OHSU, and OCCRI launch $4.5 million climate-health research partnership

*Portland, Oregon - February 11, 2026.* The Lewis & Clark
Foundation, Oregon Health & Science University, and the Oregon
Climate Change Research Institute today announced a three-year,
$4.5 million research partnership focused on climate-health
intersections in Oregon. The partnership will fund research on
wildfire-smoke exposure and respiratory health, vector-borne
disease range shifts, and heat-related mortality in
agricultural-worker populations across the Willamette Valley and
the Columbia Basin.

The Lewis & Clark Foundation committed $1.5 million across three
years. OHSU committed $2 million from its Office of Research
discretionary fund. The Oregon Climate Change Research Institute
contributed $1 million through a redirected federal NOAA grant.

"Climate-health research is one of the few areas where a
foundation our size can make a measurable contribution to a
larger field, and OHSU and OCCRI are the right partners to do it
with," said Helena Bauer, president of the Lewis & Clark
Foundation. "The science is happening at OHSU. The climate
modeling is happening at OCCRI. The foundation's contribution is
funding the connection between them."

The partnership is structured as a co-funded research consortium
with formal data-sharing protocols, quarterly published findings,
and an annual public symposium. The first symposium will be held
at the OHSU Marquam Hill campus in October 2026 and will be open
to journalists, public-health practitioners, and the public.

The research agenda was set jointly by faculty leads from each
institution. Dr. Catherine Lao at OHSU's School of Public Health
will lead the wildfire-smoke and respiratory health work. Dr.
Marcus Friedlander at OCCRI will lead the climate-modeling work
that feeds projections into the health research. A third research
line on heat-related mortality in agricultural-worker populations
will be co-led by faculty at the OHSU-PSU School of Public Health
and the Oregon State University Department of Crop and Soil
Science.

The foundation's contribution flows from the climate-adaptation
research line in the foundation's 2026 budget, which was approved
by the foundation board in December 2025. The board's approval
included an explicit reaffirmation that the foundation will not
condition funding on donor preferences over research agenda or
publication; this language was added at the request of OHSU's
research compliance office and is now standard in foundation
research-partnership agreements.

The first research findings are expected in late 2026. The
partnership's data-sharing protocol commits all three institutions
to publishing peer-reviewed work in open-access venues where
journal policies allow.
