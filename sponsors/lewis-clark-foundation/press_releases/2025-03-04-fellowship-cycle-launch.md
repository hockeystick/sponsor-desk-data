# Lewis & Clark Foundation opens 2025 Public Service Fellowship cycle

*Portland, Oregon - March 4, 2025.* The Lewis & Clark Foundation
today opened applications for the 2025 cohort of the Public Service
Fellowship program, with 18 two-year fellowships available to
graduate students entering Lewis & Clark Law School or the
Graduate School of Education and Counseling. Fellows receive an
annual stipend of $42,000 plus 50 percent tuition remission and
commit to two years of public-sector or nonprofit work in Oregon
following graduation.

The 2025 cycle will accept applications through May 2. Decisions
will be announced by June 13. Selected fellows begin coursework in
August 2025.

"The fellowship is small and selective, and that has been the
right shape for it since 2014," said Charles Kawamoto, executive
director of the Lewis & Clark Foundation. "Eighteen fellows per
year, two-year commitments, real stipends. The program produces
people who actually take public-interest jobs in Oregon and stay
in them, which is the outcome the donors funded for."

The fellowship is structured around four practice areas:
environmental and natural-resources law, education policy and
school administration, immigration and refugee services, and rural
public-defender work. Each practice area receives between three
and six fellowship slots per cohort, with the specific allocation
set annually by a committee of program faculty and foundation
staff.

A 2024 program review by an outside evaluator found that 84
percent of fellows from the 2014-2022 cohorts completed their
two-year post-graduation commitment in Oregon, and 61 percent
remained in Oregon-based public-interest work three years after
graduation. The review recommended an increase to the stipend
amount to address metro-Portland cost-of-living pressure; the
foundation board approved a stipend increase to $44,000 for the
2026 cycle but held the 2025 stipend at $42,000 due to budget
timing.

The foundation has confirmed that the 18-fellow cohort size will
be maintained for 2026 despite a 22 percent increase in
applications between 2023 and 2024. "The constraint is not the
applicant pool," Kawamoto said. "The constraint is the field
supervision capacity at partner organizations. We are not
expanding past the number of placements that can be properly
supervised."

Application materials are available at lcfoundation.org/fellowships
or through the law school's career services office and the
Graduate School's financial aid office.
