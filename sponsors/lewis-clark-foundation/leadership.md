# Leadership - Lewis & Clark Foundation

_This sponsor is a fictional analogue. Names, leadership, programs, and figures are invented for synthetic-data purposes and are not affiliated with any real company or organization._

The foundation is led by a small senior team operating under the
direction of an eleven-member Board of Trustees. The senior team
has been stable since 2022.

## Dr. Helena Bauer, Foundation President

Bauer has led the foundation since 2019. Before assuming the
presidency she served as Dean of Lewis & Clark Law School from
2011 to 2018, where she oversaw the Environmental Law Clinic's
expansion from three staff attorneys to five and the establishment
of the Public Service Fellowship program in 2014. She holds a BA
from Williams College, a JD from Yale Law School, and a PhD in
legal philosophy from Stanford. She continues to teach a small
upper-level seminar at the law school on legal ethics in
public-interest practice.

In a September 24, 2025 statement following the board's
reaffirmation of clinic governance, Bauer said: "Case selection at
a law clinic is the responsibility of the supervising attorneys,
not of the foundation that funds the endowment. The clinic was
established under a 1989 governance agreement that preserves that
independence. We will not amend it because a case became
politically uncomfortable."

## Charles Kawamoto, Executive Director

Kawamoto joined the foundation in 2017 and was promoted to
Executive Director in 2021. Before joining the foundation he was
associate vice president for development at Reed College and,
before that, spent twelve years in fundraising at Oregon Health &
Science University. He holds a BA from Willamette University and
manages the foundation's development operations, donor relations,
and the Riverbend center capital campaign closeout.

In a September 19, 2025 dedication-day statement, Kawamoto said:
"The Riverbend center took eleven years from the first donor
conversation to the ribbon cutting. The donors who carried this
project were patient. The clinic clients who needed the building
were not, and we owe them better turnaround on the next capital
project."

## Dr. Priya Anand, Director of the Environmental Law Clinic Endowment

Anand joined the foundation in 2022. She supervises endowment
disbursements to the clinic and serves as the foundation's liaison
to the law school's Office of the Dean. Before joining the
foundation she was a senior attorney at the Pacific Environmental
Advocacy Center, where she litigated water-quality cases under the
Clean Water Act. She holds a BA from the University of California,
Berkeley and a JD from Lewis & Clark Law School. She does not hold
case-selection authority for the clinic; that authority sits with
the clinic's supervising attorneys under the 1989 governance
agreement.

In an October 2025 panel at the Pacific Northwest Environmental
Law Conference in Eugene, Anand said: "An environmental law clinic
that does not occasionally take politically uncomfortable cases is
not doing the work. The 1989 governance agreement was written by
people who understood that."

## Margaret O'Reilly, Chief Investment Officer

O'Reilly has served as CIO since 2020. Previously a portfolio
manager at the Oregon State Treasury and, before that, an analyst
at a Seattle-based investment management firm. She holds a BA from
the University of Oregon and an MBA from the University of
Washington. The foundation's investments are managed in-house with
support from an external advisor under a contract last renewed in
2023.

## Tomás Hidalgo, Director of First-Generation Programs

Hidalgo has directed the First-Generation Programs office since
2021. He was a first-generation student at Lewis & Clark himself
(class of 2009), returned to the college in 2015 as an academic
advisor, and was promoted to director in 2021. He holds an MA in
higher education administration from Portland State University.

In a November 2025 statement to the campus newspaper, Hidalgo said:
"The emergency aid fund running out by week three is a structural
problem, not an unusual year. The foundation's December decision
to expand the fund is a real response. We will see whether $475,000
holds the line through spring."

## Decision-makers for sponsorship and partnership decisions (2026)

The foundation does not buy paid media sponsorships in the
conventional sense. Its public-facing communications are handled
through the college's communications office under a service
agreement, and its programmatic partnerships with media outlets are
typically structured as grants or co-funded research arrangements
rather than as ad-style sponsorship buys.

For partnership decisions:

- **Below $25,000 (event sponsorship, conference underwriting,
  small co-funded projects)**: executive director sign-off.
- **$25,001 to $150,000 (program partnerships, research co-funding,
  multi-year content collaborations)**: foundation president and
  executive director joint approval, with quarterly board summary.
- **$150,001 to $750,000 (major program partnerships, capital
  contributions to partner institutions)**: full board approval at
  one of three scheduled annual meetings.
- **Above $750,000**: full board approval plus a written rationale
  filed with the annual report. Multi-year commitments at this
  level require a co-signature from the college's board of trustees
  under the foundation-college operating agreement.

For research partnerships specifically, the foundation will not
condition funding on donor preferences over research agenda or
publication. The board reaffirmed this policy in December 2025.
