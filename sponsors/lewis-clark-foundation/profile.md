# Lewis & Clark Foundation

_This sponsor is a fictional analogue. Names, leadership, programs, and figures are invented for synthetic-data purposes and are not affiliated with any real company or organization._

The Lewis & Clark Foundation is the independent endowment foundation
of Lewis & Clark College, a private liberal arts college and law
school based in southwest Portland, Oregon. The foundation was
incorporated in 1973 as a 501(c)(3) supporting organization to hold
and steward the college's endowment, which it does under a written
operating agreement with the college's board of trustees.

The foundation's endowment was approximately $312 million as of the
June 30, 2025 fiscal year-end, up from $284 million the prior year.
The foundation distributed $15.1 million to the college and to
designated public-interest programs in fiscal year 2025, of which
roughly $9 million flowed to college operations (financial aid,
faculty support, library) and $6.1 million flowed to designated
program endowments including the Environmental Law Clinic Endowment,
the Public Service Fellowship program, and the First-Generation
Programs office.

## Programs

- **Environmental Law Clinic Endowment**: a designated $48 million
  endowment supporting the law school's environmental law clinic,
  including six staff attorneys, three clinical fellows, and
  approximately 24 law students per academic year. The clinic
  represents low-income clients and public-interest plaintiffs in
  environmental litigation, with a regional focus on the Columbia
  River Basin and the Oregon Coast.
- **Public Service Fellowship program**: 18 two-year fellowships per
  cohort, providing a $42,000 annual stipend plus partial tuition
  remission to graduate students at Lewis & Clark Law School and
  the Graduate School of Education and Counseling. Fellows commit to
  two years of public-sector or nonprofit work in Oregon following
  graduation.
- **First-Generation Programs**: a $1.8 million annual program line
  supporting first-generation undergraduate students at Lewis &
  Clark College, including dedicated advising staff, summer-bridge
  programming, and emergency-aid funds. Approximately 22 percent of
  the undergraduate class identifies as first-generation as of the
  2024-25 academic year.
- **Climate-adaptation research partnerships**: program-level
  funding for joint research between the college's Environmental
  Studies department and the Oregon Climate Change Research
  Institute (OCCRI) at Oregon State University. Active research
  projects in 2025-26 include drought-vulnerability mapping in the
  Willamette Valley and forest-health modeling on the eastern
  Cascades.
- **Riverbend Environmental Law Center**: the new $34 million
  building completed in September 2025, housing the law clinic, a
  public-interest law library, and shared workspace for visiting
  scholars. The capital project was funded primarily by a $22
  million lead gift and approximately $8 million in foundation
  reserves.

## Leadership

- **Dr. Helena Bauer**, Foundation President since 2019. Previously
  served as Dean of Lewis & Clark Law School from 2011 to 2018.
  Holds a BA from Williams College, a JD from Yale Law School, and
  a PhD in legal philosophy from Stanford.
- **Charles Kawamoto**, Executive Director. Previously associate vice
  president for development at Reed College; before that, twelve
  years in fundraising at Oregon Health & Science University. Holds
  a BA from Willamette University.
- **Dr. Priya Anand**, Director of the Environmental Law Clinic
  Endowment. Joined the foundation in 2022. Previously a senior
  attorney at the Pacific Environmental Advocacy Center; before
  that, in private practice in Seattle.

The foundation's board has eleven voting members: four appointed by
the college's board of trustees, four at-large members elected by
the foundation board, and three faculty representatives elected by
the college and law school faculty bodies.

## Position on contested issues

In April 2025 the Environmental Law Clinic took on representation of
a coalition of Columbia River Gorge orchard growers and a
farmworkers' advocacy organization, Pineros y Campesinos Unidos del
Noroeste, in a lawsuit against the Oregon Department of Agriculture
over alleged underenforcement of pesticide-drift regulations in
Hood River and Wasco counties. The plaintiffs are seeking a writ of
mandamus compelling the agency to issue rule revisions that have
been pending since 2022.

The case generated political pushback. Three state legislators sent
a letter to the foundation board in July 2025 questioning whether a
private college's law clinic, which receives indirect benefit from
state-level higher-education funding, should sue a state agency. A
local agribusiness trade group filed a separate complaint with the
college's accreditor. The foundation board reviewed the matter at
its September 2025 meeting and reaffirmed the clinic's case-selection
independence by a 9-2 vote.

Foundation president Helena Bauer, in a public statement following
the vote, framed the decision as one of clinic governance: "Case
selection at a law clinic is the responsibility of the supervising
attorneys, not of the foundation that funds the endowment. The
clinic was established under a 1989 governance agreement that
preserves that independence. We will not amend it because a case
became politically uncomfortable."

The clinic's representation continues. The case is pending in
Marion County Circuit Court.
