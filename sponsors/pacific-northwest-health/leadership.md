# Leadership — Pacific Northwest Health

_This sponsor is a fictional analogue. Names, leadership, programs, and figures are invented for synthetic-data purposes and are not affiliated with any real company or organization._

This is an internal sales document for Sponsor Desk staff. All
quoted material is drawn from public statements — press releases,
on-the-record interviews, system-issued staff letters that the
system has confirmed are non-confidential, and publicly available
regulatory filings.

## Dr. Aniko Reyes, President and Chief Executive Officer

Reyes is an internal-medicine physician who joined Pacific Northwest
Health in 2017 as Chief Medical Officer of the Beaverton hospital,
moved to system CMO in 2020, and was named President and CEO in
March 2023. Before joining the system she was CMO at a regional
health system in Spokane. She holds an MD from OHSU and a BA in
biology from Reed College. In a January 2026 statement to staff on
the unresolved ONA negotiations, Reyes wrote that "the issues on the
table are real, and we have not closed them" — a framing that
several outlets, including The Cascade Tribune, contrasted with the
system's public posture during the October 2024 strike.

## Dr. Marcus Whitfield, Chief Medical Officer

Whitfield is an internist with subspecialty training in palliative
care. BA from the University of Oregon; MD from OHSU; residency at
Legacy Emanuel. He joined Pacific Northwest Health in 2014 as a
hospitalist and was named system CMO in May 2024. Whitfield led the
internal review that produced the system's 2025 charity-care
auto-eligibility process. In a public statement at the time, he
described the prior process as "structurally inadequate" and said
the system's failure to screen patients automatically had been
"a fixable problem we did not fix soon enough."

## Diane Ostrander, Chief Operating Officer

Ostrander joined Pacific Northwest Health as COO in 2022. Previously
COO of a 14-hospital community network in Boise. MHA from Portland
State; BA in economics from Willamette University. Ostrander has led
the Emergency Department Capacity Plan since its 2024 announcement
and acknowledged in a December 2025 interview that the plan was
"behind where I expected it to be at this point" — 28 of 44
committed beds delivered, against an end-of-2026 target.

## Kwame Adjei, Chief Nursing Officer

Adjei joined Pacific Northwest Health in 2007 as a staff nurse on
a medical-surgical floor at the Northwest Portland hospital. He
moved into nursing leadership in 2013, served as Chief Nursing
Officer for the Beaverton facility from 2019 to 2023, and was named
system CNO in October 2023. BSN and MSN from OHSU. Adjei has been
the system's primary public voice on nurse staffing during the
ongoing ONA negotiations and has acknowledged on the record that
charge-nurse differentials and floor ratios are "the issues we
have not solved."

## Rachel Kim, Vice President for Communications and Public Affairs

Kim joined the system in 2021. Previously communications director
at the Oregon Health Authority, where she handled press during the
state's COVID-19 vaccine rollout. BA in journalism from the
University of Oregon. Kim is the senior approver for sponsorship
relationships, including the system's underwriting agreement with
The Cascade Tribune health desk.

## Decision-makers for sponsorship decisions (2026)

For underwriting agreements, beat sponsorships, and event-tier
relationships, Rachel Kim is the operational lead and final approver
for any package below $250,000. Above that threshold the System
Communications Committee — chaired by Kim, with Reyes, Ostrander,
and the system's chief financial officer as members — reviews and
approves at its quarterly meeting. The Cascade Tribune health desk
underwriting renewal will be reviewed at the April 2026 committee
meeting. For event-only sponsorships under $25,000, Kim's deputy
(Senior Director of Public Affairs) has signing authority.
