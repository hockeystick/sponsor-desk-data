# Cascade Behavioral Health Center opens Phase 1 in Lents, adds 32 crisis stabilization beds

*Portland, Oregon — September 23, 2025.* Pacific Northwest Health
opened the first phase of the Cascade Behavioral Health Center this
morning at SE 92nd Avenue and SE Foster Road in the Lents
neighborhood, bringing 32 crisis stabilization beds online and
beginning a phased build-out that will add 46 additional inpatient
beds and an outpatient clinic by the end of 2026.

The $94 million facility was financed through a combination of
system capital, a $28 million Multnomah County contribution, and
$14 million in state funding administered through the Oregon Health
Authority. The project was first announced in early 2023 and entered
construction in October 2023 after a Lents-neighborhood community
review process that ran for approximately five months.

"This facility exists because Multnomah County and the state
identified a specific gap and asked us to fill it," CEO Aniko Reyes
said. "Phase 1 today is 32 beds. That number is meaningful, and it
is also not enough on its own. The build-out is paced because
staffing is paced. We will not open beds we cannot staff safely."

The center accepts both walk-in patients and law-enforcement and
EMS-initiated holds, and is operating under a Memorandum of
Understanding with the Portland Police Bureau and Multnomah County
Sheriff's Office for protocols on patient handoff. The facility's
medical director, Dr. Selena Akinyi, was previously a senior
attending at OHSU's psychiatric emergency service.

The Lents siting reflects an explicit request from Multnomah County
that the facility be located in East Portland rather than in a
central or near-westside location. Pacific Northwest Health committed
during the 2023 community review to operating the facility in
coordination with the Lents Neighborhood Association and to
publishing quarterly community-impact reports beginning in Q1 2026.

Phase 2, which adds 46 additional beds and the outpatient clinic, is
scheduled for late 2026. Pacific Northwest Health acknowledged that
construction is currently three weeks behind the original Phase 2
schedule, citing staffing-recruitment timing rather than construction
delays.

"The bed count is the part that gets reported," Chief Operating
Officer Diane Ostrander said. "The harder problem is staffing them.
That is what we will be working on between now and Phase 2."
