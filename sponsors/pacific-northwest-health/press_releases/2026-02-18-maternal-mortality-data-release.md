# Pacific Northwest Health publishes first-year maternal outcomes report; system acknowledges Black and Indigenous patients still experiencing worse outcomes

*Portland, Oregon — February 18, 2026.* Pacific Northwest Health
released today the first full-year outcomes report of its Maternal
Health Initiative, a $22 million five-year program launched in
January 2024. The report shows that severe maternal morbidity rates
among Black and Indigenous patients in the system remain
substantially higher than the system average, despite a small
overall improvement in the system-wide rate.

The report, prepared with an external review panel that included
representatives from the Oregon Health Authority and two community
maternal-health organizations, covers approximately 14,800 births
across the system in calendar year 2025. The system-wide severe
maternal morbidity rate fell from 86 per 10,000 deliveries in 2023
to 79 per 10,000 in 2025. The rate among Black patients was 142
per 10,000; among Indigenous patients, 128 per 10,000.

"We are publishing this report because the alternative — sitting on
it until the numbers improve — is not available to us," CEO Aniko
Reyes said in prepared remarks at the release. "The system-wide
improvement is real and small. The disparity is also real, and it
is not getting smaller as quickly as our interventions assumed it
would."

The report identifies three interventions the system funded in 2024
and 2025: a home-visit doula program now operating in five Portland
ZIP codes, a fourth-trimester clinic at the Northwest Portland
hospital, and an internal severe-morbidity case-review process. The
report notes that the doula program reaches an estimated 38% of
eligible patients in its service area and that the system has not
yet established a plan to extend it to Beaverton or Hillsboro.

"The interventions are working where they reach patients," Chief
Medical Officer Marcus Whitfield said. "The honest reading of this
report is that they are not yet reaching enough patients."

The Maternal Health Initiative will publish outcomes data annually.
The 2026 report will include outcomes from the new fourth-trimester
clinic site at the Beaverton hospital, scheduled to open in May.

The full report is posted at pnwhealth.org/maternal-health-2025.
