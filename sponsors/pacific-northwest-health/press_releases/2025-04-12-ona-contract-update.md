# Pacific Northwest Health and Oregon Nurses Association extend contract talks; system acknowledges unresolved staffing issues

*Portland, Oregon — April 12, 2025.* Pacific Northwest Health and the
Oregon Nurses Association announced today that the two parties have
agreed to a 60-day extension of their current collective bargaining
agreement while contract negotiations continue. The extension, which
preserves existing wages and working conditions through June 11,
follows six months of bargaining that began after the October 2024
strike at three of the system's Portland-area hospitals.

In a statement issued alongside the extension announcement, Pacific
Northwest Health acknowledged that several of the issues at the
center of the talks remain unresolved. The statement identified
nurse-to-patient ratios on medical-surgical floors, charge-nurse
differential structure, and the timing of float-pool deployments as
"areas where the parties have not reached agreement and where the
system recognizes the underlying concerns are real."

"We are not going to characterize these talks as further along than
they are," Chief Nursing Officer Kwame Adjei said. "Our nurses came
out of the 2024 strike with specific staffing concerns, and those
concerns have not been addressed to their satisfaction or, frankly,
to mine. We are continuing to negotiate in good faith, and we are
committing publicly to a contract that names ratios on the floors
where ratios are the issue."

The Oregon Nurses Association, in its own statement, acknowledged
the extension while reiterating that no agreement on the central
staffing issues had been reached. ONA bargaining team chair Lisa
Pham said the union appreciated the system's willingness to extend
the contract without changes but added that "an extension is not a
resolution, and our members understand the difference."

Pacific Northwest Health declined to specify a timeline for
resolution and said it would not negotiate publicly. The next
mediated bargaining session is scheduled for April 28.

The system employs approximately 4,200 nurses represented by ONA
across nine of its 12 hospitals. The October 2024 strike, which
lasted five days, was the longest nursing strike in the system's
history.
