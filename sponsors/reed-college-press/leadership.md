# Leadership — Reed College Press

_This sponsor is a fictional analogue. Names, leadership, programs, and figures are invented for synthetic-data purposes and are not affiliated with any real company or organization._

This is an internal sales document for Sponsor Desk staff. Quoted
material is drawn from public statements: press releases, on-the-
record interviews, the press's annual report, and panel remarks at
public industry events.

## Dr. Helen Yoshida, Director

Yoshida has directed Reed College Press since 2018. Before joining
the press she was acquisitions editor at Oregon State University
Press for nine years. She holds a PhD in English from Reed College
and a BA from Lewis & Clark. In a 2021 statement marking the press's
expansion of the First Nations Imprint, Yoshida described the
press's first three decades as "institutionally narrow in ways that
we are still working to correct" — a framing she has reiterated in
panel remarks at the 2024 and 2025 Oregon Library Association
conferences.

## Theo Marquez, Editor-in-Chief and Director of Acquisitions

Marquez joined the press in 2020 as a senior editor and was named
Editor-in-Chief in 2023. Previously a senior editor at Tin House
Books, where he worked for seven years on its fiction list. MFA in
fiction from Lewis & Clark. Marquez is the editorial lead on the
Cascade Letters anthology and on the spring 2026 Vern Rutsala
posthumous collection. In a November 2025 interview with The
Oregonian, he described regional publishing as "a discipline of
saying yes to the right small thing rather than yes to the wrong
big one."

## Beatrice Whitewing, Editor for Indigenous Literature

Whitewing is a member of the Confederated Tribes of Grand Ronde and
has led the First Nations Imprint since 2019. Previously senior
editor at the University of Oregon Press, where she acquired the
Northwest Native Voices series. BA in English from Willamette
University. Whitewing is the press's primary public voice on the
imprint's editorial review structure and on the royalty-rate
commitment, which she described in a 2024 panel at the AWP
conference as "not a generosity but a baseline."

## Owen Halloran, Operations and Distribution Manager

Halloran joined the press in 2016 and has managed its distribution
relationships, print scheduling, and warehouse operations since
2019. Previously operations coordinator at Powell's Books, in the
used-book buying division. BA from Portland State.

## Marisol Vega, K-12 Program Director

Vega leads the Title I Books to Schools program. Previously a
school librarian at Madison High School in Northeast Portland and a
program officer at the Oregon Library Association. MLIS from the
University of Washington. Vega has been the public face of the
press's K-12 program since the program's expansion in 2023.

## Decision-makers for sponsorship decisions (2026)

For sponsorships, underwriting agreements, and event-tier
relationships, Helen Yoshida is the final approver for any
commitment that touches the press's editorial program (book
launches, anthology partnerships, author tours). Marisol Vega is
the operational lead and approver for sponsorships tied to the K-12
program. For purely operational sponsorships under $15,000, Owen
Halloran has signing authority. Beatrice Whitewing has consulting
review on any sponsorship that involves First Nations Imprint
titles or authors; her review is required, not advisory.

  conference: "Regional publishing is a slow project. The
  catalogue is the brand."
- Marquez, November 2025 interview with The Oregonian: "A
  discipline of saying yes to the right small thing rather than
  yes to the wrong big one."
- Whitewing, March 2024 AWP conference panel on Indigenous

- Vega, August 2025 statement at the Title I program rollout
