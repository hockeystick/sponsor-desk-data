# Reed College Press announces fall 2025 catalogue: 12 titles spanning Oregon poetry, labor history, and Indigenous fiction

*Portland, Oregon — August 26, 2025.* Reed College Press announced
its fall 2025 catalogue today, with 12 new titles publishing between
September and December. The list includes three titles in the
Oregon Voices Reissue Series, two titles from the First Nations
Imprint, the 2025 edition of the Cascade Letters anthology, and
six new acquisitions across poetry, fiction, and regional
nonfiction.

The fall list's anchor reissue is a long-out-of-print 1972 collection
by the Eugene poet Vern Rutsala, prepared with a new editorial
introduction by the poet Kim Stafford. The reissue precedes a
spring 2026 publication of Rutsala's collected late poems, prepared
in coordination with the poet's estate. The Oregon Voices Reissue
Series is funded in part by a 2022 grant from the Oregon Cultural
Trust.

"The Rutsala reissue is the kind of work the press exists to do,"
Editor-in-Chief Theo Marquez said. "The book has been out of print
for a generation. Bringing it back, and pairing it with a new
introduction by an Oregon poet of Stafford's standing, is a more
useful contribution than most of what publishing tries to do at
larger scales."

The fall list's First Nations Imprint titles include a story
collection by the Warm Springs writer Adeline Suppah and a new
edition of a 1978 oral history collection from the Grand Ronde
cultural office. Both titles were prepared under the imprint's
community editorial review structure, which gives the partner
tribes final approval on titles by their members.

"These are not press-led projects," Editor for Indigenous
Literature Beatrice Whitewing said. "The Suppah collection was
acquired by Warm Springs and brought to us. Our role is editorial
support and distribution. The work is the partner tribes' work."

The 2025 edition of the Cascade Letters anthology, edited by the
novelist Mitchell Jackson, includes new work from 14 emerging
Oregon writers. The anthology will be sold through trade channels
and provided free to all 47 Title I schools currently enrolled in
the press's Books to Schools program.

The full fall 2025 catalogue is available at
reedcollegepress.org/fall-2025.
