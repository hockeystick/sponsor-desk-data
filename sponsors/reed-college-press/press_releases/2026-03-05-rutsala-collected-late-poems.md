# Reed College Press to publish Vern Rutsala's collected late poems in April; first complete edition prepared with the poet's estate

*Portland, Oregon — March 5, 2026.* Reed College Press will publish
the collected late poems of the Eugene poet Vern Rutsala in April
2026, the press announced today. The volume, titled The Late Poems
1998–2014, is the first complete edition of Rutsala's late work and
was prepared in coordination with the poet's estate over a
four-year editorial process. The initial print run is 4,000 copies.

Rutsala, who taught at Lewis & Clark for nearly four decades and
who died in 2014, is widely regarded as one of the central poets of
the Oregon literary tradition. His earlier collections, including
The Window (1964) and Walking Home from the Icehouse (1981),
remain in print; the late poems, written in the final 16 years of
his life, have been available only in scattered magazine
publications and archival collections.

"The Late Poems is the work the press has been moving toward
since 2018," Director Helen Yoshida said. "Vern Rutsala's late
work has been read by very few people, and a fair number of them
are poets who know it from photocopies. Bringing the poems together
in a single volume, in coordination with the estate, is the most
useful thing we can do."

The volume was edited by Theo Marquez and the Reed College
emeritus professor Pauls Toutonghi, with afterwords from four
contemporary Oregon poets including Kim Stafford and Crystal
Williams. The fall 2025 reissue of Rutsala's 1972 collection, also
under the press's Oregon Voices Reissue Series, was published in
preparation for the April release.

"Vern Rutsala's late work has been in storage long enough,"
Marquez said. "We are publishing the volume in the form the estate
wanted, at a price the regional audience can afford, with afterwords
from poets who actually read him. Those are the editorial decisions
that matter."

The Late Poems 1998–2014 will be available through Consortium Book
Sales and Distribution and at independent bookstores across the
Pacific Northwest. The press will host a launch reading at Powell's
City of Books in late April; the date will be announced separately.
