# Reed College Press expands Books to Schools partnership; 16,000 books to 54 Title I schools in 2025–26

*Portland, Oregon — November 14, 2025.* Reed College Press announced
today an expansion of its Title I Books to Schools program, with a
projected distribution of 16,000 books to 54 Title I schools across
five Oregon counties during the 2025–26 school year. The expansion
adds seven schools — three in Hood River County and four in
Clackamas County — to the 47 schools served in 2024–25.

The program is funded by a $480,000 three-year grant from the
Powell's Community Foundation, now in its second year. Books are
selected by participating school librarians from a curated list of
roughly 180 in-print titles drawn from the press's regional
catalogue and a small number of co-distributed titles from partner
publishers. Selections are not approved by the press; the press's
role is to fulfill librarian orders and absorb the cost of titles
provided through the program.

"The program works the way it works because librarians ask for the
titles they want," K-12 Program Director Marisol Vega said. "We
publish a list, librarians choose, we send the books. The press
does not pick for them. That is the part of the program that other
versions of K-12 publishing partnerships sometimes get wrong."

The expansion includes a partnership update with the Oregon Library
Association, which is administering professional-development
workshops for participating librarians on regional Oregon literature
and on integrating Indigenous-authored titles into classroom
collections. The workshops are funded separately by the Oregon
Cultural Trust and are open to librarians at non-participating
schools at no charge.

The program does not currently extend to Lane, Deschutes, or
Jackson counties despite requests from librarians in those
districts. The press's 2026 program plan identifies Lane County as
the next expansion target, contingent on a renewal of the Powell's
Community Foundation grant at the end of the current grant period.

"We have been honest with librarians in Lane County about what we
can and cannot commit to," Vega said. "The grant renewal is the
gating item. We are not going to announce an expansion we cannot
fund."

A list of participating schools is posted at
reedcollegepress.org/books-to-schools.
