# Leadership — Travel Oregon

_This sponsor is a fictional analogue. Names, leadership, programs, and figures are invented for synthetic-data purposes and are not affiliated with any real company or organization._

Travel Oregon is governed by a nine-member Tourism Commission
appointed by the Governor and confirmed by the Senate. Day-to-day
management sits with an executive team of four, supported by a
staff of approximately 65 across the Salem and Portland offices.

## Aaron Beckwith, CEO and Director

Beckwith was appointed by the Tourism Commission in March 2023
following an eight-month national search. He came to the role from
the Oregon Cultural Trust, where he served as executive director
for six years; before that, he was deputy director at the Idaho
Division of Tourism. Beckwith holds a BA in public policy from
Lewis & Clark College.

In February 2025 testimony before the House Committee on Economic
Development, Beckwith said the agency's historical
marketing-spend distribution was "not defensible on its merits"
and that the rural-allocation floor adopted in the 2025–27 budget
was "overdue rather than generous."

## Lila Whitehorse, Director of Industry Affairs

Whitehorse is a citizen of the Confederated Tribes of Warm
Springs. She joined Travel Oregon in 2022 after seven years as
executive director of Travel Pendleton, and earlier, three years
as a program officer with the Affiliated Tribes of Northwest
Indians economic-development arm. Whitehorse leads the agency's
work on the Oregon Tribal Tourism Initiative.

In a September 2025 statement at the Initiative's first-cycle
grant announcement, Whitehorse said: "These projects were
designed by tribal cultural-resources offices, not by Travel
Oregon. Our role is to fund and to stay out of the way."

## Marcus Voigt, Chief Strategy Officer

Voigt joined Travel Oregon in 2021 from the Oregon Business
Development Department, where he was deputy director for
research. He oversees the agency's research and economic-impact
work, including the biennial Oregon Travel Impacts report. Voigt
holds an MPP from the Evans School at the University of
Washington.

In an October 2025 interview with the Oregon Business Journal,
Voigt said: "The Oregon Coast lodging market is supply-bound on
peak weekends. Marketing more nights into Tillamook in July is not
the work. Building inland alternatives is the work."

## Renata Ofori, Communications Director

Ofori joined Travel Oregon in 2024 from the Oregon Health
Authority, where she was communications director through the
COVID-19 response. Earlier in her career she was a reporter at
OPB. She leads the agency's media relations, brand, and
records-request response.

In a May 2025 statement following the settlement of the Pell ORS
192 litigation, Ofori said: "The records should have been
released in substantially less redacted form the first time. The
revised workflow makes that the default going forward."

## Hank Aldrich, Chair, Tourism Commission

Aldrich owns and operates Deschutes Whitewater, a Bend-based
rafting outfitter. He was appointed to the Tourism Commission in
2023 and elected chair in January 2024. He represents Central
Oregon on the Commission.

## Decision-makers for sponsorship and grant decisions (2026)

For agency programmatic spending and grant-making, approval
authority is set by procurement threshold and program type, not by
sponsorship size. Direct-award grants under $50,000 are approved
by the relevant program director (Industry Affairs for tourism
operators, Strategy for research, Communications for media
partnerships). Direct-award grants between $50,000 and $250,000
require sign-off from the CEO and the program director jointly.
Grants over $250,000 require Tourism Commission action at a public
meeting.

Competitive grant cycles (Rural Tourism Studio, the Tribal
Tourism Initiative grant pool, the Accessibility Project capital
grants) follow published RFP processes with rubric-based scoring;
final award decisions are ratified by the Commission. Marketing
underwriting agreements with media outlets, including The Cascade
Tribune, are signed by the Communications Director under
delegated authority from the CEO and are reported to the
Commission as part of the quarterly budget update.
