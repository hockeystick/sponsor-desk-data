# Travel Oregon releases 2024 coastal tourism impact update; rural-county receipts lag metro

*Salem, Oregon — April 22, 2025.* Travel Oregon today released its
2024 Coastal Tourism Impact Update, the agency's annual readout
on visitor spend and lodging receipts across Oregon's seven
coastal counties. The report shows total coastal visitor spend of
$2.34 billion in 2024 — a 3.1% increase over 2023 — but with
sharp variance by county and a continued gap between large lodging
operators and small independent ones.

Tillamook County reported $312 million in 2024 visitor spend, up
4.2% year over year. Lincoln County reported $498 million, up
1.8%. Curry County, the southernmost coastal county, reported
$94 million in visitor spend — flat against 2023 and below the
county's 2019 pre-pandemic peak of $107 million. Coos County
reported $186 million, up 2.9%.

The report's small-operator survey, conducted in coordination
with the Oregon Coast Visitors Association, found that 41% of
independent lodging operators in Curry, Coos, and southern
Lincoln counties reported lower 2024 occupancy than in 2023, while
larger operators (50+ rooms) in the same counties reported flat
to modestly improved occupancy.

"The headline number is recovery; the operator-level data is
not," CEO Aaron Beckwith said. "A small motel in Port Orford and
a 200-room property in Lincoln City are not in the same market.
Reporting only the aggregate misses what is actually happening on
the southern coast."

The agency announced two responses tied to the 2025–27 biennial
budget. The Oregon Coast Visitor Recovery Fund will allocate
$2.6 million of its $4.6 million biennial total to operators with
fewer than 25 rooms, with a priority for operators in Curry and
Coos counties. The Industry Affairs grant cycle opening in May
2025 will include a $400,000 set-aside for southern-coast
destination management organizations.

Chief Strategy Officer Marcus Voigt noted that the report's
methodology was revised in 2024 to break out operator-size
categories, a change requested by coastal chambers of commerce in
2023. The full report, including county-level tables and the
small-operator survey instrument, is available on Travel Oregon's
open data portal.
