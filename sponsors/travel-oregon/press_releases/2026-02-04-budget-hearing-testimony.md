# Travel Oregon CEO acknowledges legislative scrutiny on rural funding equity in budget testimony

*Salem, Oregon — February 4, 2026.* Travel Oregon CEO Aaron
Beckwith testified yesterday before the Joint Committee on Ways
and Means Subcommittee on Natural Resources, presenting the
agency's mid-biennium budget update and answering more than
ninety minutes of questions from legislators on rural funding
distribution, the Oregon Tribal Tourism Initiative, and the
agency's revised public-records workflow.

The hearing was the agency's first formal appearance before the
subcommittee since the 2025 session and drew sustained
questioning from Eastern Oregon legislators on whether the 40%
rural-allocation floor adopted in the 2025–27 biennial budget
is being met at the program level.

Beckwith testified that the floor is being met in aggregate but
acknowledged variance by program. The Rural Tourism Studio cohort
allocation is currently at 67% rural; the Industry Affairs grant
pool is at 44%; the Accessibility Project capital grants are at
38% — short of the floor. He committed to publishing
program-level rural-share figures quarterly beginning in the Q3
2026 budget update.

"The aggregate number is on track. The Accessibility Project
number is not, and I'm not going to talk around that," Beckwith
said. "The next round of capital grants closes in May. We will
report what the rural share looks like, and we will publish
methodology."

Subcommittee co-chair Sen. Lynn Findley (R-Vale) pressed Beckwith
on whether the agency's revised public-records workflow, adopted
following the May 2025 settlement of the Pell ORS 192 litigation,
has changed agency operating costs. Beckwith testified that the
workflow has added approximately $180,000 in annualized staff time
to the Communications office but has reduced records-request
response time from a 90-day median to a 21-day median.

Communications Director Renata Ofori, testifying after Beckwith,
said the agency will move to publishing all marketing-contractor
invoices over $10,000 on the open data portal beginning April
2026, with vendor names unredacted.

The subcommittee did not vote on the agency's mid-biennium
budget request and is expected to take it up alongside the broader
natural-resources budget package later in the session.
