# Travel Oregon awards first-cycle grants under Oregon Tribal Tourism Initiative

*Grand Ronde, Oregon — September 15, 2025.* Travel Oregon today
announced the first programmatic grant awards under the Oregon
Tribal Tourism Initiative, totaling $1.8 million across six
tribally led tourism projects. The announcement was made at the
Confederated Tribes of Grand Ronde's Cultural Center, with
representatives from each of the nine federally recognized Oregon
tribes attending.

The Initiative was established in 2024 under formal MOUs between
Travel Oregon and each of the nine tribes: the Burns Paiute
Tribe; the Confederated Tribes of the Coos, Lower Umpqua and
Siuslaw Indians; the Confederated Tribes of Grand Ronde; the
Confederated Tribes of Siletz Indians; the Confederated Tribes of
the Umatilla Indian Reservation; the Confederated Tribes of Warm
Springs; the Coquille Indian Tribe; the Cow Creek Band of Umpqua
Tribe of Indians; and the Klamath Tribes. The MOU structure
places programmatic direction with the tribes' cultural-resources
offices and Travel Oregon in an administrative role.

Six projects received first-cycle awards. The Confederated Tribes
of Grand Ronde will receive $385,000 to expand interpretive
programming at the Chachalu Tribal Museum and Cultural Center.
The Confederated Tribes of Siletz Indians will receive $310,000
for a coastal interpretive trail in partnership with Lincoln
County Parks. The Confederated Tribes of Warm Springs will
receive $295,000 for a tribal artisan-marketplace pilot. Three
additional projects, at the Burns Paiute Tribe, the Klamath
Tribes, and the Coquille Indian Tribe, received awards in the
$200,000–$310,000 range.

"These projects were designed by tribal cultural-resources
offices, not by Travel Oregon," said Director of Industry Affairs
Lila Whitehorse, a citizen of the Confederated Tribes of Warm
Springs. "Our role is to fund and to stay out of the way."

CEO Aaron Beckwith said the Initiative will be included in the
agency's 2027–29 biennial budget request as a permanent line
item with a proposed $4.0 million biennial floor, replacing the
two-year sunset language under which the Initiative was
originally funded.

The full grant docket, including each project's scope and
reporting cadence, is available on the agency's open data
portal.
