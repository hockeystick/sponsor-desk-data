# Leadership — Powell's Community Foundation

_This sponsor is a fictional analogue. Names, leadership, programs, and figures are invented for synthetic-data purposes and are not affiliated with any real company or organization._

This document profiles senior leadership and decision-makers at Powell's Community Foundation. It is intended for sponsor-facing sales conversations and is internal only. Quotes are drawn from public statements and the foundation's annual reports.

## Margaret Hsu, Executive Director

Executive Director since 2021. Previously director of the Wallace Foundation's arts education program for six years; before that, deputy director at Literary Arts in Portland. PhD in education policy from the University of Oregon; MA in English from Reed College. Hsu has led the foundation's spending up from 4.8% to its current 5.5–6.0% target and was the public face of the 2025 reading-list controversy.

In her March 2025 board statement on the reading-list complaints, Hsu framed the foundation's position plainly: "Reading lists curated by educators are educator decisions. The foundation's role is to fund, not to curate. We will not establish a precedent of removing titles from a list because a funder received complaints about them."

## Jonathan Forsythe, Director of Programs

Joined Powell's CF in 2022. Previously program officer at the Meyer Memorial Trust for eight years; before that, library director at the Hood River County Library system. MLIS from the University of Washington; BA in history from Lewis & Clark College. Forsythe leads program design across the four grantmaking areas and chairs the cross-program review committee.

In an October 2025 Oregon Library Association keynote, Forsythe addressed the K–12 librarian funding gap: "The math is unforgiving. Oregon spends less per K–12 student on school librarians than 38 other states. The foundation's 2026 cycle prioritizes that gap because it is the gap most likely to compound over a decade if left alone."

## Anjali Mehta, Chief Financial Officer

Joined Powell's CF in 2020. Previously controller at Oregon Community Foundation; before that, audit manager at Moss Adams. CPA; MBA from Portland State University. Mehta oversees the endowment ($120M), grants administration, and compliance.

In a January 2026 board treasurer's report, Mehta noted that the foundation's spending policy was being reviewed: "The 5.5–6.0% target served us well during 2022–2024. We are reviewing whether 2026 conditions — particularly the planned third year of the journalism sustainability program — call for a temporary uplift to 6.5%."

## Theo Briarwood, Communications Director

Joined Powell's CF in 2023. Previously communications director at the Native American Youth and Family Center; before that, freelance journalist covering Pacific Northwest civic news. BA in English from Reed College. Briarwood manages external communications, including the foundation's response to the Parents First Oregon campaign.

## Eleanor Powell, Board Member

Powell family representative on the foundation board since 2019. One of two Powell family seats per the foundation's bylaws. Eleanor Powell is also a board member of the Oregon Cultural Trust and previously served as treasurer of Literary Arts.

## Decision-makers for sponsorship decisions (2026)

Powell's CF is more typically a *funder* than a sponsorship buyer — it underwrites journalism rather than purchasing display advertising or branded content. Decisions for journalism partnerships flow through Jonathan Forsythe's office. For partnerships under $50,000, Forsythe has standing approval authority. Between $50,000 and $200,000 require approval by the cross-program review committee, which meets monthly. Above $200,000 require board approval at the next quarterly meeting (March, June, September, December).

The foundation's pass-through grants for local journalism support are administered with editorial firewalls maintained by the recipient organization — the foundation does not participate in editorial decisions and does not ask to.
