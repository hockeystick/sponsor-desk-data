# Leadership — Cascadia Credit Union

_This sponsor is a fictional analogue. Names, leadership, programs, and figures are invented for synthetic-data purposes and are not affiliated with any real company or organization._

This document profiles senior leadership and decision-makers at Cascadia Credit Union. It is intended for sponsor-facing sales conversations and is internal only. Quotes are drawn from public statements, the credit union's annual reports, and member letters.

## Eric Carlsen, President and Chief Executive Officer

President and CEO since 2019. Previously CEO of Iowa Community Credit Union for seven years; before that, regional president at USAA Federal Savings Bank. MBA from the University of Iowa; BA in economics from Grinnell College. Carlsen led Cascadia's expansion to a community charter market footprint and the 2024 launch of the Sustainable Mortgage product.

In a December 2025 letter to members, Carlsen addressed the NCUA Document of Resolution directly: "We took a calculated bet on a fintech partnership that moved faster than our internal controls could supervise. The examiners were right to flag it. We're fixing it on the timeline we committed to, and we're publishing our progress in writing because that's what members deserve."

## Maria Lopez, Chief Marketing Officer

Joined Cascadia in 2022. Previously VP for member experience at Patelco Credit Union in Northern California; before that, director of marketing at OnPoint Community Credit Union. BA in communications from Portland State University. Lopez leads brand, member acquisition, and the credit union's media partnerships.

In a November 2025 Oregon Credit Union Association panel, Lopez talked about the difference between credit-union marketing and bank marketing: "Banks sell products. We tell members what their cooperative did with their money last quarter. Those are different stories and they require different voices."

## Robert Yu, Chief Lending Officer

Twenty-six years at Cascadia in progressively senior lending roles. Began as a loan officer in 1999; named CLO in 2017. BA in finance from Oregon State University. Yu oversees the Sustainable Mortgage program and the First-Time Homebuyer Bridge.

In a February 2026 Cascadia Annual Meeting presentation, Yu spoke to the criticism that the Sustainable Mortgage product favors higher-income members: "The program was designed for households who could afford the up-front cost of certification. The critique is fair. We're publishing borrower-income distribution data starting Q3 2026 because the data should be the basis of any next conversation."

## Hannah Reichert, Chief Risk and Compliance Officer

Joined Cascadia in 2024 in the wake of the NCUA Document of Resolution. Previously head of compliance at Beneficial State Bank; before that, examiner at the NCUA's Pacific region office (the same regional office that issued the DOR). JD from the University of Oregon. Reichert leads the third-party risk program rebuild that the DOR remediation requires.

## William Hayes, Board Chair

Volunteer board member since 2014; elected Chair in 2022. Retired Multnomah County circuit court judge. Hayes also serves on the Oregon Health Authority's behavioral health advisory committee.

## Decision-makers for sponsorship decisions (2026)

Sponsorship decisions at Cascadia flow through Maria Lopez's office. For partnerships under $20,000, Lopez has standing approval authority. Between $20,000 and $75,000 require approval by the marketing committee. Six-figure partnerships ($100,000+) require board approval at the next quarterly meeting (February, May, August, November).

Cascadia is particularly receptive to media partnerships that align with the credit union's three civic priorities: financial literacy, first-time homebuyer support, and Latino-community outreach in East Portland and the Willamette Valley. Partnerships outside those priorities are unusual.
