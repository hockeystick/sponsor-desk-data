# Leadership — Portland General Energy

_This sponsor is a fictional analogue. Names, leadership, programs, and figures are invented for synthetic-data purposes and are not affiliated with any real company or organization._

This document profiles senior leadership and decision-makers at Portland General Energy. It is intended for sponsor-facing sales conversations and is internal only. Quotes are drawn from public statements, regulatory filings, and earnings calls; bios are drawn from public records and the company's own communications.

## Sara Holloway, Chief Executive Officer

Joined Portland General Energy in 2014 as Vice President of Power Operations. Promoted to Senior Vice President of Operations in 2018, then named CEO in February 2022. Holds a BS in electrical engineering from Oregon State University and an MBA from Willamette University. Previously held operations roles at PacifiCorp and the Bonneville Power Administration.

In a December 2025 letter to customers, Holloway addressed the September 2025 rate filing directly: "Filing a rate increase nine months after publishing the reliability report we did is uncomfortable, and we want to acknowledge that. Customers are better served by us filing the case in full than by phasing it into smaller increments that obscure what's actually being paid for."

## Vincent Chau, Vice President for Public Policy and Regulatory Affairs

Joined PGE in 2019. Previously deputy general counsel at the Oregon Public Utility Commission for nine years. JD from Lewis & Clark Law School; BA in political science from Reed College. Chau leads PGE's regulatory strategy at the OPUC and on federal matters before FERC and the Bonneville Power Administration.

At the November 2025 OPUC stakeholder workshop, Chau publicly accepted that the rate filing's timing was politically difficult: "We're not asking the commission to ignore that the filing comes after a damaging reliability report. We're asking the commission to consider whether the work this filing pays for is the right work to do."

## Carmen Delgado, Vice President for Communications

Joined PGE in 2023. Previously Director of Communications at Travel Oregon for five years; before that, communications manager at Oregon Public Broadcasting. BA in journalism from the University of Oregon. Delgado leads external communications, regulatory PR, and PGE's media partnerships portfolio.

In a January 2026 internal memo later reported by Willamette Week, Delgado wrote: "The point of underwriting is not to buy quiet. We accept that editorial will continue to publish critical pieces on our rate filings. We continue to fund the work because the work matters."

## Marcus Steiner, Chief Financial Officer

Joined PGE in 2020. Previously CFO at Avista Utilities; before that, controller at Pacific Gas and Electric. CPA; MBA from the University of Washington. Steiner is the primary point of contact for institutional investor relations and rate-case financial testimony.

In December 2025 OPUC docket testimony, Steiner gave a frank assessment of the substation modernization program: "Two of the three eastern Multnomah substation upgrades came in 14% over budget. We are not asking ratepayers to absorb that overrun without explanation."

## Charlene Buckley, Board Chair

Independent director since 2018; named Chair in 2024. Previously CEO of Cascade Health Solutions and a director of the Oregon Business Council. Buckley chairs the board's compensation and audit committees.

## Decision-makers for sponsorship decisions (2026)

Sponsorship and underwriting decisions flow through the Communications Committee, which Carmen Delgado chairs. The committee meets monthly. For partnerships under $25,000, Delgado has standing approval authority. Partnerships between $25,000 and $100,000 require the Committee's written approval. Six-figure partnerships ($100,000+) require sign-off from the CEO and a presentation to the board's compensation committee. The OPB Energy & Environment Underwriting (currently $180,000 annually) is reviewed annually by the full Communications Committee.

For media partnerships specifically, the primary point of contact is Delgado's office; for editorial-firewall questions involving rate-related coverage, decisions involve Vincent Chau's regulatory team because rate matters trigger separate compliance review.
