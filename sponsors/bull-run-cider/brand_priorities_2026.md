# 2026 Brand Priorities — Bull Run Cider

_This sponsor is a fictional analogue. Names, leadership, programs, and figures are invented for synthetic-data purposes and are not affiliated with any real company or organization._

_Internal document for the founder team and external regional
media partners. Approved by Tess Iverson and Jonas Briar, January
2026._

2026 is the first full production year after the November 2024
atmospheric-river event has finished working through inventory.
Wholesale pricing has moved up modestly. Supply mix has shifted.
Distribution is expanding north into Washington. The 2026
communications plan is built around describing those facts
straightforwardly, not around generating contrast with them.

## 1. Hood River agritourism partnership through Travel Oregon and the Chamber

The three-year cooperative marketing agreement signed in February
2024 with Travel Oregon and the Hood River Chamber of Commerce
runs through February 2027. The 2026 deliverables include four
co-promoted shoulder-season weekends (April, May, October,
November), a co-funded fall-color campaign, and continued presence
in the Hood River Saturday Market when the market is in season.

## 2. Single-orchard storytelling — Sunny Slope and Smiley Hollow co-branded releases

The 2026 releases will continue the single-orchard label series.
The Sunny Slope October 2026 release will be the first to include
fruit from the partner-orchard expansion announced in November
2025. The Smiley Hollow Eola-Amity Hills release returns for its
fourth year. Communications around both should credit the
named orchards specifically and disclose varietal blends.

## 3. Wholesale expansion into the San Juan Islands and Olympic Peninsula

The Washington wholesale expansion announced in March 2026 adds
roughly 18 new accounts across Friday Harbor, Lopez, Orcas, Port
Townsend, Sequim, and the Olympic Peninsula coast. The
communications plan supports the launch with one regional press
release, three taproom-to-taproom co-launches with named
Washington partners, and a print run of point-of-sale collateral
for the new accounts.

## 4. Organic transition reporting — quarterly progress against 30%-by-2027

The 2025 figure was 17% certified organic by fruit volume against
a 30%-by-2027 commitment. The 2026 plan is to publish quarterly
progress to the company's website with the partner-orchard
certification status named, and to disclose any setbacks in the
same channel rather than only the gains.

## 5. Talk straight about climate's effect on Hood River fruit

The 2025 atmospheric river damage and the underlying shift in
chill-hour patterns in the Mid-Columbia have been part of how
Briar and Iverson talk about the company in public. The 2026
guidance is to keep doing that — in release notes, in interviews,
and in the wholesale price-list explanation when prices move.

## What we will not do

- We will not run "craft" or "artisanal" brand campaigns that
  paper over the 2026 price increase. The price moved because the
  fruit cost moved.
- We will not pretend the November 2024 atmospheric river was a
  one-off. The pattern of late-fall extreme precipitation events
  in the Gorge is part of the operating environment now.
- We will not pursue national distribution in 2026. Bull Run is
  a Pacific Northwest cidermaker and remaining one is a strategic
  choice, not a holding pattern.
