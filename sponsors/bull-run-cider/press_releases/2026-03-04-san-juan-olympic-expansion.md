# Bull Run Cider expands wholesale distribution into Washington's San Juan Islands and Olympic Peninsula

*Hood River, Oregon — March 4, 2026.* Bull Run Cider today
announced the expansion of its Washington wholesale distribution
into the San Juan Islands and the Olympic Peninsula, adding
approximately 18 new retail and on-premise accounts across Friday
Harbor, Lopez Island, Orcas Island, Port Townsend, Sequim, Forks,
and adjacent communities. The expansion is effective March 2026
and is supported by the company's existing Washington
distribution partner, Mt. Baker Beverage.

Named launch accounts include the San Juan Island Bottle Co. in
Friday Harbor, the Madrone Tap House in Eastsound on Orcas, Port
Townsend's Pourhouse, and the Sequim Cider Cellar. A full account
list is being shared with each named partner ahead of point-of-sale
materials shipping.

"We are not trying to be in every Washington county," said
Garrett Knox, Bull Run's Sales Director. "We are trying to be in
the bottle shops and taprooms where the buyers will come back and
ask us how the harvest went. The San Juans and the Olympic
Peninsula fit that picture."

The expansion adds approximately 4,200 cases of annual case
volume on a full-year basis, primarily across the year-round core
line: Bull Run Dry, Pine Grove Heritage, and Gorge Sessions.
Single-orchard limited releases (Sunny Slope and Smiley Hollow)
will be allocated to a subset of the new accounts in case volumes
similar to the company's existing Seattle-area allocation.

Three taproom-to-taproom co-launch events are scheduled across
March and April 2026. The first is at Madrone Tap House on Orcas
on March 22, paired with a visit from head cidermaker Jonas
Briar. The second is at Pourhouse in Port Townsend on April 5.
The third is at the Sequim Cider Cellar on April 19.

Bull Run's distribution remains entirely within Oregon and
Washington following the expansion. The company has not pursued
California, Idaho, or out-of-region wholesale relationships and
has stated as part of its 2026 brand-priorities planning that it
will not do so in 2026.

The launch is the first wholesale expansion the company has
undertaken since the November 2024 atmospheric river event and
the resulting 2025 harvest decline. CEO Tess Iverson said in the
release that the expansion was made possible by the multi-year
supply agreement signed with Sunny Slope Orchards in November
2025, which, together with the existing Smiley Hollow contract,
underwrites volume planning through 2028.
