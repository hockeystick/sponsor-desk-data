# Bull Run Cider releases 2025 fall harvest update; reports yield declines on heritage blocks following November 2024 atmospheric river

*Hood River, Oregon — October 8, 2025.* Bull Run Cider today
released its 2025 fall harvest update, reporting yield declines
across several heritage-apple blocks at partner orchards in the
Hood River Valley and the Eola-Amity Hills. The update is the
sixth annual harvest disclosure the company has published since
beginning the practice in 2020.

Yield figures for the 2025 harvest, against the rolling
five-year average for each block:

- Sunny Slope Orchards Newtown Pippin block (Pine Grove): down
  41%.
- Sunny Slope Orchards Bartlett-adjacent dessert block: down 28%.
- Smiley Hollow Farms Roxbury Russet block (Eola-Amity Hills):
  down 19%.
- Smiley Hollow Farms Ashmead's Kernel block: down 12%.
- Composite contract-fruit volume from non-named partner orchards:
  down 22%.

The company attributes the declines to a combination of physical
damage from the November 2024 atmospheric river event — which
flooded orchard floors across the Hood River Valley and toppled
fruit before it had been picked — and a chill-hour deficit in
February and March 2025 that left several heritage varietals
under-developed at bloom.

"The Newtown Pippin block at Sunny Slope is down 41% on yield
this year," said Jonas Briar, Bull Run's co-founder and head
cidermaker. "Some of that is the November 2024 storm. Some of it
is chill hours we did not get in February and March. Both things
are true at once."

The company expects the 2025 harvest decline to affect fruit
availability for two of its single-orchard limited releases. The
October 2025 Sunny Slope release will be a smaller-than-usual
bottling (3,800 cases against a typical run of 6,400). The
November 2025 Smiley Hollow release will run at roughly 80% of
the prior-year volume.

Bull Run's year-round core line — Bull Run Dry, Pine Grove
Heritage, and Gorge Sessions — will not see a volume reduction in
2026 distribution. The company has shifted varietal blends in two
of the three core products to reflect the available 2025 supply.
The shift is disclosed on the back labels and on the company's
website.

The 2026 wholesale price list, distributed to accounts on October
1, includes a 6% price increase on the core line. The release of
the price list included a written explanation citing fruit cost
movement specifically, rather than a general inflation framing.

The full harvest update, including methodology and block-by-block
notes, is available at bullruncider.com/harvest-2025.
