# Bull Run Cider and Sunny Slope Orchards announce multi-year heritage-varietal supply agreement through 2028

*Hood River, Oregon — November 19, 2025.* Bull Run Cider and
Sunny Slope Orchards today announced a four-year written supply
agreement covering heritage-apple varietals from Sunny Slope's
Pine Grove blocks. The agreement runs from the 2025 harvest
through the 2028 harvest and locks in fruit prices at agreed
levels across the term.

Sunny Slope Orchards is the third-generation Iverson family
orchard in the Pine Grove neighborhood east of the city of Hood
River. Bull Run co-founder and CEO Tess Iverson grew up on the
orchard before the 2009 founding of Bull Run. The two operations
have had a continuous handshake supply relationship since Bull
Run's founding; today's announcement formalizes that relationship
in writing for the first time.

Under the agreement, Sunny Slope will supply contracted volumes
of Newtown Pippin, Roxbury Russet, Ashmead's Kernel, and a
small block of Esopus Spitzenburg from designated blocks. Bull
Run commits to per-pound prices that hold across the four-year
term, with adjustments only for documented input-cost changes
above defined thresholds.

"Sunny Slope is the orchard I grew up on. Putting a written
multi-year agreement in place — at prices that hold through 2028
— is the right way for this company to talk about supporting
Hood River fruit," Iverson said. "Handshake worked. The orchard
deserved a contract too."

The agreement does not change the ownership of either business.
Sunny Slope remains owned and operated by the Iverson family
(currently by Tess Iverson's parents, Bjorn and Linda Iverson, with
day-to-day management by orchard manager Caleb Vargas). Bull Run
remains owned by Tess Iverson and Jonas Briar.

The companies also announced an organic-transition co-investment
of $180,000 over the four-year term, funded by Bull Run, that
will support Sunny Slope's transition of approximately 22 acres
to USDA-certified organic production. Two of those acres are
projected to certify in calendar year 2026; the remaining 20
acres are scheduled across 2026 and 2027.

The agreement does not affect Sunny Slope's existing fresh-market
relationships, which continue to account for the majority of the
orchard's annual fruit volume.
