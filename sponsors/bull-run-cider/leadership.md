# Leadership — Bull Run Cider

_This sponsor is a fictional analogue. Names, leadership, programs, and figures are invented for synthetic-data purposes and are not affiliated with any real company or organization._

Bull Run is a privately held Oregon LLC owned by its two
founder-operators, Tess Iverson and Jonas Briar, with a small
employee equity pool established in 2019. The named leaders below
are the company's full senior team.

## Tess Iverson, Co-founder and CEO

Third-generation Hood River orchardist; grew up on Sunny Slope
Orchards in Pine Grove, east of the city of Hood River. Worked the
family orchard from her teens through 2009. Co-founded Bull Run
Cider with Jonas Briar in 2009. Handles distribution agreements,
capital structure, orchard partnerships, and the company's
relationship with Travel Oregon and the Hood River Chamber. BA in
agricultural economics, Oregon State University.

## Jonas Briar, Co-founder and Head Cidermaker

Trained in fermentation through Washington State University's
Wine Science program (certificate, 2007); worked seasonal orchard
labor across the Mid-Columbia from 2002 through 2009. Co-founded
Bull Run with Iverson in 2009. Runs production at the Pine Grove
facility and is the company's public voice on heritage-apple
varietals and the climate-driven shift in PNW orcharding.

## Anita Park, Operations Manager

Joined Bull Run in 2017 from Full Sail Brewing, where she had
worked in production scheduling and taproom operations from 2011
to 2017. Oversees the taproom, the farm-dinner series, the live
music calendar, and Hood River Saturday Market scheduling.

## Garrett Knox, Sales Director

Joined Bull Run in 2021 from Reverend Nat's Hard Cider in
Portland, where he had been wholesale account manager for
Washington from 2018 to 2021. Manages the Oregon and Washington
wholesale book and led the planning for the 2026 San Juan Islands
and Olympic Peninsula expansion.

## Decision-makers for sponsorship decisions (2026)

- Sponsorship engagements above $10,000 require sign-off from
  Tess Iverson. She is the sole final approver above that
  threshold.
- Taproom programming sponsorships (live music, the farm-dinner
  series, Saturday Market visibility) are signed by Anita Park up
  to $5,000.
- Wholesale-channel sponsor partnerships (taproom-to-taproom
  co-launches, point-of-sale collateral co-funding) are signed by
  Garrett Knox up to $5,000; above that threshold, joint with
  Iverson.
- Editorial sponsorships that touch the orchard partnership story
  or the climate-driven supply story are reviewed by Briar before
  any commitment.

## What they've said publicly (recent)

Jonas Briar, in the October 2025 fall harvest update: "The
Newtown Pippin block at Sunny Slope is down 41% on yield this
year. Some of that is the November 2024 storm. Some of it is
chill hours we did not get in February and March. Both things are
true at once."

Tess Iverson, in a November 2025 statement announcing the multi-year
supply agreement with Sunny Slope Orchards: "Sunny Slope is the
orchard I grew up on. Putting a written multi-year agreement in
place — at prices that hold through 2028 — is the right way for
this company to talk about supporting Hood River fruit."

Garrett Knox, in a March 2026 trade interview about the Washington
expansion: "We are not trying to be in every Washington county.
We are trying to be in the bottle shops and taprooms where the
buyers will come back and ask us how the harvest went. The San
Juans and the Olympic Peninsula fit that picture."

Anita Park, in a May 2025 Hood River News profile of the
farm-dinner series: "The dinners are not a marketing thing for
us. They pay for themselves at $85 a seat and they are how the
taproom builds a year-round calendar instead of a summer one."
