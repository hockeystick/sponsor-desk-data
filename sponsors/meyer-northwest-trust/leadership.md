# Leadership - Meyer Northwest Trust

_This sponsor is a fictional analogue. Names, leadership, programs, and figures are invented for synthetic-data purposes and are not affiliated with any real company or organization._

The trust is led by a small senior team operating under the direction
of a nine-member Board of Trustees. The senior team is undergoing a
planned transition through March 2026.

## Eleanor Whitcomb, President and CEO (retiring March 2026)

Whitcomb has led the trust since 2014. Before joining Meyer
Northwest Trust she was program director at the Northwest Health
Foundation and, before that, a program officer at the Ford
Foundation working on civic participation in the western United
States. She holds a BA from Reed College and a PhD in public policy
from Portland State University. She announced her retirement in
November 2025 and will remain on the board as a non-voting trustee
emerita through 2027.

In a November 12, 2025 statement, Whitcomb said: "Twelve years is
long enough. The trust needs a leader who is going to make the next
ten years of decisions, not finish the last ten years of mine."

## Martin Iron Cloud (Yakama), President-elect

Iron Cloud joined the trust in 2018 as Program Director for Tribal
and Rural Programs and was promoted to Vice President in 2022. He
will assume the presidency on March 16, 2026. Before joining the
trust he was program director at Ecotrust, where he led the
organization's Native foods initiative, and policy director at the
Affiliated Tribes of Northwest Indians. He holds a BA from the
University of Oregon and a Master of Public Administration from the
Harvard Kennedy School.

In a November 12, 2025 statement, Iron Cloud said: "I am taking this
job because the trust's relationship with the Confederated Tribes of
Warm Springs has been built on the right principle - that tribal
priorities are set by tribal governments, not by funders. I want to
extend that principle across the rest of the portfolio."

## Dr. Renata Okafor, Vice President for Equity Initiatives

Okafor leads the Black-led nonprofit operating support fund and the
trust's equity-evaluation work. She joined the trust in 2020 after
seven years at the Northwest Health Foundation, where she was
associate director for community partnerships. She holds a BA from
Spelman College and an EdD from the University of Oregon.

In a March 2025 panel at the Council on Foundations regional
meeting in Seattle, Okafor said: "Operating support is what
nonprofits ask for. Project grants are what funders prefer. The gap
between those two preferences is where capacity gets eroded."

## Daniel Schreiber, Chief Financial Officer

Schreiber has served as CFO since 2017. Previously controller at the
Oregon Community Foundation. He holds a BA from Reed College and an
MBA from Willamette University. Schreiber oversees the trust's
investment program, which is managed by an external investment
office under a five-year contract last renewed in 2024.

## Yvette Tanaka, Vice President for Communications and Public Affairs

Tanaka joined the trust in 2022. She was previously a senior reporter
at Oregon Public Broadcasting covering state government and, before
that, an editor at The Oregonian's business desk. She holds a BA
from the University of Washington.

## Decision-makers for grant decisions (2026)

Grant decisions are organized by approval threshold rather than by
sponsorship tier. The trust does not buy media sponsorships in the
conventional sense; its journalism funding flows through general
operating grants to newsrooms, and editorial firewall language is
standard in every grant agreement.

- **Up to $50,000**: program staff approval, with quarterly summary
  to the board. Program directors hold sign-off authority within
  their portfolios. For journalism grants, the program director is
  Yvette Tanaka.
- **$50,001 to $250,000**: senior staff approval, with the
  president/CEO as final signatory. Decisions are reviewed quarterly
  by the board's Program Committee.
- **$250,001 to $1,000,000**: full board approval at one of four
  scheduled annual meetings. The board's Program Committee reviews
  in advance and brings a recommendation.
- **Above $1,000,000**: full board approval plus a written rationale
  filed with the trust's annual report. Multi-year commitments above
  this threshold trigger the September 2026 transition-window joint
  review by Whitcomb and Iron Cloud.

For journalism grants specifically: the trust will not enter a grant
agreement with any newsroom that does not adopt the trust's standard
firewall language. The language was last revised in 2024 in
consultation with the Local News Foundation.

## What they've said publicly (recent)

- **Whitcomb, November 12, 2025 (retirement announcement):** "The
  next president should not inherit a planning process I started.
  Major program decisions for 2027 and beyond will be made by Martin
  and his team."
- **Iron Cloud, October 2025 (Affiliated Tribes of Northwest Indians
  annual meeting, Pendleton):** "Funder-defined priorities are how
  tribal communities have been ill-served for fifty years. The
  Regional Resilience Fund works because the tribes set the agenda."
- **Okafor, March 2025 (Council on Foundations regional meeting):**
  "If you do not give operating support, you are telling nonprofits
  you do not trust them to run their organizations. That is the
  honest read."
- **Tanaka, August 2025 (Oregon Newspaper Publishers Association
  conference, Bend):** "We do not direct editorial. If a newsroom we
  fund publishes a story critical of the trust, that is the system
  working. We will not retaliate, and we have a written record going
  back to 2023 of not retaliating."
- **Whitcomb, May 2025 (statement on the independent review):** "We
  applied the policy correctly. We communicated badly. Both of those
  things are true and the second one was on me."
