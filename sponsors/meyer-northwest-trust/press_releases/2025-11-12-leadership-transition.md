# Eleanor Whitcomb to retire as president of Meyer Northwest Trust; Martin Iron Cloud named successor

*Portland, Oregon - November 12, 2025.* Meyer Northwest Trust today
announced that President and CEO Eleanor Whitcomb will retire
effective March 16, 2026, after eleven years leading the
foundation. The trust's board of trustees has named Martin Iron
Cloud, currently vice president for tribal and rural programs, as
president-elect. Iron Cloud will assume the role on March 16.

Whitcomb, 64, has led the trust since 2014. During her tenure the
endowment grew from approximately $510 million to $850 million,
the trust launched the Black-led nonprofit operating support fund
and the multi-year journalism sustainability program, and annual
grantmaking grew from $26 million to $48 million.

"Twelve years is long enough," Whitcomb said. "The trust needs a
leader who is going to make the next ten years of decisions, not
finish the last ten years of mine. Martin is the right person at
the right time."

Iron Cloud, an enrolled member of the Yakama Nation, joined the
trust in 2018 as program director for tribal and rural programs
and was promoted to vice president in 2022. Before joining the
trust he was program director at Ecotrust and policy director at
the Affiliated Tribes of Northwest Indians. He holds a BA from the
University of Oregon and a Master of Public Administration from
the Harvard Kennedy School.

"I am taking this job because the trust's relationship with the
Confederated Tribes of Warm Springs has been built on the right
principle - that tribal priorities are set by tribal governments,
not by funders," Iron Cloud said. "I want to extend that principle
across the rest of the portfolio."

The succession was planned. The board began a formal CEO search in
March 2025 with a national search firm and conducted finalist
interviews in September. The board considered five finalists,
including two internal candidates and three external candidates;
the search committee's unanimous recommendation went to the full
board on November 4.

Board chair Marisol Vega, a former Oregon Supreme Court justice,
will lead a six-month transition oversight committee. The
committee will jointly review with both leaders any multi-year
grant commitment above $500,000 that extends through 2028. The
arrangement is one-time and ends September 2026.

Whitcomb will remain on the board as a non-voting trustee emerita
through 2027 to support continuity on multi-year initiatives. She
will not hold operating authority after March 16, 2026.

The trust's 2026 grantmaking calendar, including the third year of
the journalism sustainability program and the second cycle of the
Regional Resilience Fund, will proceed on the schedule announced
in October 2025. Iron Cloud confirmed in a follow-up briefing with
program staff that no 2026 program-line changes are planned.
