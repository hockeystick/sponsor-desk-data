# Meyer Northwest Trust and Oregon Community Foundation announce $12 million Regional Resilience Fund

*Portland, Oregon - April 15, 2025.* Meyer Northwest Trust and the
Oregon Community Foundation today announced the Regional Resilience
Fund, a $12 million pooled grantmaking vehicle for climate
adaptation in rural Oregon. The fund will distribute grants over
three years across Wasco, Hood River, Sherman, and Jefferson
counties, with a separate parallel allocation administered in
partnership with the Confederated Tribes of Warm Springs natural
resources branch.

Meyer Northwest Trust contributed $7.5 million to the fund. The
Oregon Community Foundation contributed $4.5 million. The two
organizations will co-administer the grantmaking through a joint
program committee, with separate review for the Warm Springs
allocation conducted by tribal natural resources staff under a
written agreement that places programmatic priorities with the
tribes.

"Climate adaptation in rural Oregon is not a single-issue problem,"
said Eleanor Whitcomb, president and CEO of Meyer Northwest Trust.
"Wildfire resilience, irrigation, emergency preparedness, salmon
habitat - these are tied together in the field, and they have to be
funded together. A pooled fund with two large foundations and a
tribal natural resources branch is the right structure for that."

The fund will open its first grant cycle in June 2025 with $4
million available. Eligible applicants include county and city
governments, federally recognized tribal governments, soil and
water conservation districts, watershed councils, and 501(c)(3)
organizations operating in the four named counties or on the
Warm Springs Reservation. Grant size will range from $50,000 to
$500,000.

"Rural Oregon has been told for thirty years to figure out climate
adaptation on county budgets that cannot do it," said Martin Iron
Cloud, vice president for tribal and rural programs at Meyer
Northwest Trust. "This fund is a partial answer. It is not the
whole answer. But it removes one of the constraints."

The fund's 2025 cycle will prioritize wildfire-resilience capital
projects in Wasco and Hood River counties, where the 2024 fire
season exposed gaps in water-system redundancy that county
emergency managers had identified in writing as early as 2019.

Application materials are available at
meyernorthwesttrust.org/regional-resilience-fund and at
oregoncf.org. The first deadline is June 30, 2025. Decisions will
be announced by September 15.

The fund will be evaluated in early 2027 by an independent
evaluator selected jointly by the two foundations and the tribal
natural resources branch.
