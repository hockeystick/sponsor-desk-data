# Meyer Northwest Trust opens 2026 grant cycle; commits $6 million to third year of journalism sustainability program

*Portland, Oregon - January 22, 2026.* Meyer Northwest Trust today
opened its 2026 grant cycle and confirmed a $6 million commitment
to the third year of its multi-year journalism sustainability
program. The 2026 cycle will distribute approximately $51 million
across the trust's five program areas, a modest increase from $48
million in 2025.

The journalism sustainability program continues general operating
support to seven independent Oregon newsrooms funded in 2024 and
2025 and adds a new shared business services pool, administered by
Local News Foundation, that funds back-office capacity shared
across smaller newsrooms.

"Operating support and shared services are what newsrooms have
been asking funders for since 2018," said Yvette Tanaka, the
trust's vice president for communications and public affairs.
"Project grants are not the gap. The gap is the membership
operations team a small newsroom cannot afford on its own."

The 2026 commitments to the seven previously funded newsrooms total
$3.6 million. The shared business services pool is funded at $1.4
million. The remaining $1 million supports newsroom-training
partnerships with the Agora Journalism Center at the University of
Oregon and a small newsroom-emergency reserve administered by the
trust directly.

Each newsroom grant agreement includes a written editorial
firewall. The firewall language was last revised in 2024 in
consultation with the Local News Foundation and prohibits any
funder direction over editorial decisions, including topic
selection, story timing, and publication of coverage that names or
critiques the trust itself.

Outside the journalism program, 2026 grant cycle openings include:

- The Regional Resilience Fund's second cycle, $4 million,
  applications due April 30.
- Housing equity operating grants for Hood River County and the
  Bend-Redmond corridor, $2.1 million in expansion funding,
  applications due May 15.
- Year four of the Black-led nonprofit operating support fund, no
  open application (cohort additions are by invitation through the
  fund's existing participants).
- Indigenous-led environmental program partnership with the
  Confederated Tribes of Warm Springs, programmatic priorities set
  by the tribal natural resources branch.

President Eleanor Whitcomb noted that the 2026 grant calendar was
finalized in November 2025 and will not be modified by the March
leadership transition. "The 2026 commitments are made," Whitcomb
said. "Martin will set 2027 priorities. That separation was
deliberate."

Application materials and program-area guidelines are at
meyernorthwesttrust.org/2026-cycle.
