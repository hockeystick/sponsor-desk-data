# 2026 Programmatic Priorities - Meyer Northwest Trust

_This sponsor is a fictional analogue. Names, leadership, programs, and figures are invented for synthetic-data purposes and are not affiliated with any real company or organization._

_Internal document shared with regional media partners and civic stakeholders. Approved by the Board of Trustees, January 2026._

The trust's 2026 priorities continue the program lines set in the
2024-2027 strategic plan, with one structural change reflecting the
March 2026 leadership transition. Priorities below are listed in
budget order rather than rank order.

## 1. Housing equity: expand the Hood River and Bend portfolios

Most of the trust's housing-equity funding has historically gone to
Multnomah and Washington counties. The 2026 budget shifts $2.1
million toward organizations working in Hood River County and the
Bend-Redmond corridor, where rents have risen faster than
metro-Portland and where the local nonprofit infrastructure is
thinner. Grants in this expansion are operating grants, three-year
commitments, and capped at $250,000 per organization per year.

## 2. Journalism sustainability: third year of the multi-year program

The trust will commit $6 million to Oregon journalism in 2026, the
third year of the multi-year initiative. The 2026 cycle continues
general operating support to seven independent Oregon newsrooms and
adds a new shared business services pool, administered by Local News
Foundation, that funds back-office capacity (audience research,
membership operations, ad-operations) shared across smaller
newsrooms that cannot sustain those functions individually. The
trust does not direct editorial decisions and maintains a written
firewall policy with each grantee newsroom.

## 3. Climate adaptation in rural Oregon

The Regional Resilience Fund, co-administered with the Oregon
Community Foundation, opens its second grant cycle in April 2026.
The 2026 cycle adds explicit eligibility for irrigation district
modernization in Wasco and Hood River counties, an addition
requested in writing by Oregon Water Resources Department staff
during the 2025 stakeholder review.

## 4. Black-led nonprofit operating support: enter year four

The pooled fund for Black-led nonprofit operating support enters its
fourth year of a five-year commitment. The 2026 budget adds three
funded organizations, bringing the total to 17. The trust has
committed to a public evaluation of the fund in 2027, conducted by
an independent evaluator selected by the participating
organizations rather than by the trust.

## 5. Leadership transition: maintain grantmaking continuity through March

President Eleanor Whitcomb retires in March 2026 after eleven years
in the role. President-elect Martin Iron Cloud assumes the role on
March 16. The board has approved a six-month overlap of authority on
multi-year grant renewals: any renewal decision involving more than
$500,000 in commitments through 2028 will be reviewed jointly by
both leaders during the transition window. This is a one-time
arrangement and ends September 2026.

## What we will not do

- We will not direct editorial decisions at journalism grantees.
  The firewall is written into each grant agreement and the trust
  has declined two requests in 2025 from board members to suggest
  story topics to funded newsrooms.
- We will not fund 501(c)(4) advocacy organizations or organizations
  whose principal activity is campaign-cycle electoral work. This is
  a settled policy reaffirmed by the board in May 2025 and is not
  under reconsideration in 2026.
- We will not adopt a single-issue program area to replace any of
  the five current program lines. Program-line consolidation has
  been discussed and rejected; the board judges that the current
  five-line structure better matches the foundation's geographic and
  community footprint than a more concentrated portfolio would.
