# 2026 Brand Priorities — Cascadia Outfitters Co-op (PNW Chapter)

_This sponsor is a fictional analogue. Names, leadership, programs, and figures are invented for synthetic-data purposes and are not affiliated with any real company or organization._

_Internal document for the Cascadia Chapter regional team and
external regional media partners. Approved by the Cascadia Chapter
Programming Committee, December 2025._

The Cascadia Chapter enters 2026 with two regional pressures shaping
the communications agenda. Federal discussions through 2025 raised
the prospect of rollback or shrinkage of two PNW national monument
designations the co-op has historically supported, and the labor
question has moved into the chapter with the Bellingham store
unionization vote in October 2025 and contract bargaining underway
in early 2026. The priorities below take both pressures as given
rather than around them.

## 1. Cascade-Siskiyou and Steens Mountain monument communications

The Cascade-Siskiyou National Monument and Steens Mountain
Cooperative Management and Protection Area together cover roughly
615,000 acres of southern and southeastern Oregon that the co-op's
member surveys identify as among the highest-priority public lands
for the Cascadia Chapter membership. The 2026 plan is to maintain
the existing $650,000 Public Lands Access Initiative regional
spending and to expand member-facing communications around any
federal rule-making proceedings that affect either designation.

## 2. Outdoor School for All — close the rural gear-grant gap

The Outdoor School Partnership reached 84 schools in 2025–2026 but
identified a gap in Eastern Oregon and Southwest Washington Title I
middle schools, where 23 schools that requested gear grants did not
receive them. The 2026 add: an additional $400,000 grant pool
specifically for the schools in that gap, distributed through the
same OSU Extension administrative channel.

## 3. Tribal co-stewardship pilot with the Confederated Tribes of Grand Ronde

The June 2025 announcement of a five-year co-stewardship pilot in a
12,400-acre Coast Range parcel adjacent to Tribal trust lands was
the first such pilot for the co-op. The 2026 communications plan is
to publish a one-year status update in summer 2026 written jointly
with the Tribal Council's Natural Resources Department, with credit
and authorship shared, and not to claim outcomes the pilot has not
yet produced.

## 4. Apparel sourcing transparency — publish the Tier 3 roadmap

The co-op-brand apparel line has published Tier 2 supplier lists
since 2022. The November 2025 board action approved a Tier 3
roadmap that the regional team will help publicize through chapter
member channels in Q2 2026, including a plain-language explanation
of what Tier 3 disclosure does and does not establish about working
conditions.

## 5. Be honest about the 2030 emissions miss

The co-op is at a 31% reduction against a 65%-by-2030 target. The
regional team's 2026 communications guidance is to state that
position whenever the topic arises, including in member meetings
and in any sponsored editorial coverage. No glossing.

## What we will not do

- We will not run regional brand campaigns that paper over the
  Bellingham bargaining process or imply that the co-op's
  cooperative ownership form makes the labor question moot.
- We will not underwrite editorial coverage that touches retail
  labor at the co-op's own stores. The conflict is plain.
- We will not pursue "wilderness purity" framings of public-lands
  programming that exclude or minimize Indigenous co-stewardship.
