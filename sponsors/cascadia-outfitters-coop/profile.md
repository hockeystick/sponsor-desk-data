# Cascadia Outfitters Co-op

_This sponsor is a fictional analogue. Names, leadership, programs, and figures are invented for synthetic-data purposes and are not affiliated with any real company or organization._

Cascadia Outfitters Co-op is a member-owned consumer cooperative
selling outdoor gear, apparel, and instructional programming, with
approximately 22 million members nationally and 180 retail locations
across 41 states. The co-op was founded in 1938 by a group of
Seattle-area mountaineers who pooled funds to import ice axes from
Austria at cost, and it has operated continuously as a member-owned
cooperative since then. Headquarters remain in the Seattle suburbs;
the Cascadia Chapter is the regional member chapter covering Oregon,
Washington, and Northern Idaho.

The Pearl District flagship in Portland opened in 2002 and is the
largest single retail location in the Cascadia Chapter, with 280
employees and roughly 410,000 annual visitors. The chapter also
operates eight other PNW stores (including Tigard, Hillsboro, Bend,
Eugene, Salem, Vancouver WA, Tacoma, and Bellingham), a Cascadia
Chapter office in inner Southeast Portland, and a regional
distribution facility in Sumner, Washington.

## Operations

The co-op is governed by a member-elected nine-seat National Board.
Regional chapters do not have separate boards but do run regional
programming budgets approved annually by chapter members. The
Cascadia Chapter's 2026 programming budget is $4.9 million, set at
the December 2025 chapter member meeting.

Programs in current operation in the Cascadia Chapter:

- **Cascadia Chapter Trail Fund**: $1.8 million in annual grants for
  PNW trail maintenance, distributed through the Pacific Crest
  Trail Association's Oregon-Washington regional chapters, the
  Mount Hood National Forest stewardship cooperatives, and the
  Washington Trails Association.
- **Outdoor School Partnership**: a four-year, $2.4 million
  commitment (2024–2027) for gear grants to Title I middle schools
  participating in Outdoor School for All, administered through the
  OSU Extension Service. The 2025–2026 grant cycle reached 84
  schools, including 11 in Eastern Oregon.
- **Public Lands Access Initiative**: approximately $650,000 in
  annual regional spending on policy advocacy and member
  communications related to PNW public-lands designations,
  including ongoing work on the Cascade-Siskiyou National Monument
  and Steens Mountain.
- **Apparel Sourcing Disclosure**: the co-op publishes Tier 2
  supplier lists for its co-op-brand apparel line. A Tier 3
  disclosure roadmap was approved in November 2025, with phased
  publication targeted for 2027.
- **Pearl District Flagship community programming**: free monthly
  navigation and avalanche-awareness clinics, a youth gear-library
  with 1,400 active borrower accounts, and a quarterly Cascadia
  Chapter member meeting hosted in the flagship's events room.

## Position on contested issues

In 2024 and 2025, retail workers at three Cascadia Outfitters stores
filed for union representation: a Berkeley, California store under
RWDSU (election held November 2024, voted to unionize), a Cleveland
Heights, Ohio store under UFCW (election held March 2025, narrowly
voted against), and the Bellingham, Washington store under RWDSU
(election held October 2025, voted to unionize). Bargaining for a
first contract at the Bellingham store began in January 2026 and is
ongoing.

The co-op's national leadership has stated a position of formal
neutrality during organizing campaigns. The RWDSU has criticized
specific store-level practices during the Berkeley and Bellingham
campaigns, including the timing of certain manager-led "captive
audience" meetings; the co-op's national HR group has acknowledged
the criticism in writing and committed to a revised manager training
curriculum effective Q1 2026. No campaign is currently underway at
the Pearl District flagship or at any other Cascadia Chapter store.

Regional leadership does not set national labor relations posture
and does not speak to bargaining specifics at the Bellingham store.
Regional leadership does answer questions about specific Cascadia
Chapter store practices directly and on the record.

On emissions: the co-op has a public 2030 target of a 65% absolute
reduction in scope 1 and 2 emissions against a 2018 baseline. As of
the 2025 sustainability disclosure, the co-op is at a 31% reduction
— roughly four percentage points behind the linear path. Regional
leadership has been asked to communicate that gap clearly rather
than restate the target without acknowledging current performance.
