# Leadership — Cascadia Outfitters Co-op (PNW Chapter)

_This sponsor is a fictional analogue. Names, leadership, programs, and figures are invented for synthetic-data purposes and are not affiliated with any real company or organization._

The Cascadia Chapter is a regional chapter of a national consumer
co-op. Regional leadership is appointed (not member-elected) and
reports through the co-op's national operations structure. The
named regional leaders below carry the chapter's day-to-day
authority for programming, communications, and store operations.

## Marisol Ojeda, Regional Director, Pacific Northwest

Twelve-year tenure with the co-op. Began as an assistant store
manager at the Seattle flagship in 2014, served as Director of
Store Operations for the PNW from 2018 to 2022, and was appointed
Regional Director in May 2022. BA in geography, University of
Washington. Lives in NE Portland.

## Devon Whitethorn, VP Member Experience, Pacific Northwest

Joined in October 2023 from Patagonia retail, where they had
served as regional retail director for the Mountain West. Prior to
Patagonia, ten years in REI store-operations roles in the
Intermountain region. BA in environmental studies, Lewis & Clark
College.

## Helen Akiyama, Director of Public Lands Programs, Pacific Northwest

Joined the co-op in March 2021 from Oregon Wild, where she had
been the lead campaign organizer for Cascade-Siskiyou National
Monument expansion from 2016 to 2021. JD, Lewis & Clark Law School
(2014). Lives in Ashland with regular travel to the chapter office
in Portland.

## Calvin Ross, Director of Cooperative Governance and Member Communications, Pacific Northwest

Six-year tenure. Runs the chapter's annual cooperative officer
elections, member meeting logistics, and member-facing
communications. The chapter's primary public-facing contact for
press inquiries on co-op governance and on the union question at
Cascadia Chapter stores.

## Bea Lindquist, Pearl District Flagship Store Director

Eight-year tenure with the co-op. Promoted to Flagship Store
Director in February 2024 after serving as Assistant Store Director
since 2020. Oversees the 280 Pearl District employees and the
flagship's community programming calendar.

## Decision-makers for sponsorship decisions (2026)

- Sponsorship engagements above $25,000 in regional spend require
  joint sign-off from Marisol Ojeda and Devon Whitethorn.
- Sponsorship of editorial coverage on public-lands topics requires
  separate sign-off from Helen Akiyama, who reviews for fit with
  the Public Lands Access Initiative.
- Pearl District neighborhood programming and flagship-tied
  sponsorships are signed by Bea Lindquist up to $15,000; above
  that threshold, joint with Whitethorn.
- Calvin Ross is the primary press contact for governance and
  labor-related inquiries and does not sign sponsorship
  agreements.
