# Cascadia Outfitters Co-op publishes 2025 Pearl District flagship sustainability disclosure; reports current emissions trajectory is behind 2030 target

*Portland, Oregon — February 18, 2026.* Cascadia Outfitters Co-op
today published the 2025 sustainability disclosure for its Pearl
District flagship store. The disclosure reports that the
flagship's scope 1 and 2 emissions are 24% below the store's 2018
baseline, and that the co-op as a whole is 31% below its 2018
baseline against a public 65%-by-2030 target.

Both figures are behind the linear path to the 2030 target. At a
linear pace, the co-op's 2025 reduction would be approximately
35%; the actual figure of 31% leaves the co-op roughly four
percentage points behind path. The Pearl District flagship's 24%
figure leaves it roughly seven percentage points behind path.

"We are publishing both numbers, including the gap to path, so
that the co-op's members and the publications that cover us can
hold the position accurately," said Marisol Ojeda, the co-op's
Regional Director for the Pacific Northwest. "Holding the public
target without showing where we are against it would not honor
the cooperative form."

The disclosure attributes the gap to three causes the co-op
identifies as actionable and one it does not. The actionable
causes: a delayed HVAC retrofit at the Sumner, Washington
distribution facility (now scheduled for completion in Q3 2026),
slower-than-projected adoption of refrigerant alternatives at the
Pearl District flagship's café operation, and a 2024 expansion of
the flagship's hours that increased lighting and HVAC load. The
non-actionable cause: a regional grid mix that has decarbonized
more slowly than the co-op's 2018 forecast assumed, particularly
on the Washington side of the chapter.

The disclosure also reports progress against the co-op's 2027
goal of 30% organic-fiber content in its co-op-brand apparel
line. The 2025 figure is 22%, on path to the 2027 target.

The disclosure does not revise the 2030 target. Ojeda said in the
release that the co-op is "not going to move the goalpost in a
year when we are behind it." The co-op's National Board has
asked regional leadership to publish quarterly progress reports
beginning Q2 2026.

The full disclosure, including methodology and the underlying
data, is available at cascadia-outfitters.coop/pearl-district-2025.
