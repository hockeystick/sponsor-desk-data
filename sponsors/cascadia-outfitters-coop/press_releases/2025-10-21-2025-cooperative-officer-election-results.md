# Cascadia Outfitters Co-op announces results of 2025 cooperative officer elections

*Seattle, Washington — October 21, 2025.* Cascadia Outfitters
Co-op today announced the certified results of the 2025
cooperative officer elections. Three of the nine seats on the
National Board were on the ballot. Voting closed October 14 and
results were certified by the co-op's independent elections
auditor on October 19.

National Board results:

- **Carlos Beltran**, incumbent (Seattle), reelected to a
  three-year term with 61.4% of votes cast.
- **Priya Ramaswamy**, new seat (Denver), elected to a three-year
  term with 47.8% of votes cast in a four-candidate race.
- **Aaron Stillwell**, new seat (Burlington, Vermont), elected to
  a three-year term with 39.2% of votes cast in a five-candidate
  race.

National turnout was 8.7% of eligible members, up from 7.4% in
2024. Cascadia Chapter turnout was 11.3% of eligible chapter
members, up from 9.1% in 2024 and the highest of any regional
chapter.

"Turnout this year was higher than last year and lower than what
the cooperative form deserves," said Calvin Ross, the Cascadia
Chapter's Director of Cooperative Governance and Member
Communications. "We are looking at why eligible members are not
voting and at what we are doing or not doing to make the ballot
visible."

The 2025 election was the first conducted under a revised online
voting platform adopted by the National Board in March 2025
following member feedback that the prior platform was difficult to
use on mobile devices. The auditor's certification report noted
no irregularities.

Two ballot questions also passed: a member-initiated bylaws
amendment requiring annual public disclosure of the co-op's Tier 2
apparel supplier list (passed with 78.4% support), and a board-referred
proposal to add a member-elected ombudsperson role beginning in
2027 (passed with 64.1% support).

The Bellingham, Washington store unionization vote — which the
co-op had noted in its Q3 2025 communications schedule as a
separate matter from cooperative officer elections — was
certified by the National Labor Relations Board on October 17,
2025. The Bellingham store is the co-op's first retail location
to unionize. The co-op will begin contract bargaining with the
RWDSU bargaining committee in January 2026.

Full election results, the auditor's certification report, and
the texts of both ballot questions are posted at
cascadia-outfitters.coop/2025-elections.
