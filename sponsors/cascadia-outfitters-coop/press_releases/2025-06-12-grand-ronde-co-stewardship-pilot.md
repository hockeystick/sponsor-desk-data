# Cascadia Outfitters Co-op and the Confederated Tribes of Grand Ronde announce five-year Coast Range co-stewardship pilot

*Grand Ronde, Oregon — June 12, 2025.* Cascadia Outfitters Co-op
and the Confederated Tribes of Grand Ronde today announced a
five-year co-stewardship pilot covering a 12,400-acre Coast Range
parcel adjacent to Tribal trust lands in Polk and Yamhill counties.
The pilot is the first formal co-stewardship agreement the co-op
has entered with a sovereign Tribal government.

Under the agreement, the Tribe's Natural Resources Department will
co-lead land-management planning across the parcel, including trail
siting, restoration priorities for Coast Range salmon-bearing
streams, and seasonal access planning. The co-op's role is funding
support and member-volunteer coordination through the Cascadia
Chapter Trail Fund. Total co-op funding committed across the
five-year pilot is $1.6 million.

"The Tribes have stewarded these lands for time out of mind, and
this pilot is structured around that fact," said Helen Akiyama,
the co-op's Director of Public Lands Programs for the Pacific
Northwest. "We are funding partners and we are members of the
public who shop at this co-op. We are not co-managers of Tribal
priorities."

Tribal Council Chair Marlene Riggs said the agreement was the
product of three years of conversation between the Tribes and the
co-op. "We have entered agreements like this before with federal
agencies. This is the first with a private cooperative entity. We
will be evaluating it on its own terms, and we are not in a hurry
to sign similar pilots elsewhere until we have learned from this
one."

The pilot's first-year work plan, jointly published with the
announcement, focuses on three deliverables: a co-authored
land-use baseline report (due June 2026), restoration planning for
two named salmon-bearing tributaries, and a member-volunteer
schedule that defers to the Tribes' seasonal access calendar. The
parties have committed to publishing a joint one-year status
update in summer 2026.

The announcement does not change the underlying ownership of the
parcel, which remains in mixed federal and private ownership, and
does not constitute a federal co-stewardship designation under the
Department of the Interior's Joint Secretarial Order 3403.

The full pilot agreement is available on the Tribe's website and at
cascadia-outfitters.coop/co-stewardship-pilot.
